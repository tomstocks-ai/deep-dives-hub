## 1. Company Overview

Hut 8 Corp. (NASDAQ: HUT) is a North American bitcoin miner pivoting aggressively into AI data center infrastructure. Originally founded in 2011 as a pure-play crypto miner, the company has restructured itself into a hybrid "digital infrastructure" platform with two revenue streams: **Bitcoin Mining** (self-mining and hosting) and **High-Performance Computing / AI Data Centers** (hosting GPU clusters for AI training and inference).

The company operates mining facilities across Alberta and Texas, leveraging low-cost energy contracts. Its strategic shift began in 2024–2025 with the acquisition and development of power-rich sites in Texas, culminating in two massive AI data center campuses: **River Bend** and **Beacon Point**.

## 2. Financial Analysis

### Income Statement (Q1 2026)

| Metric | Value |

| Revenue Growth | +225% YoY |

| Net Loss | (\$253.1M) |
| Unrealized Digital Asset Loss | (\$295.7M) |
| Adjusted Net (ex-crypto MTM) | ~\$42.6M profit |

The headline net loss is misleading. The \$253M loss was driven almost entirely by a \$295.7M mark-to-market write-down on bitcoin holdings due to BTC price volatility in Q1. Excluding this non-cash accounting item, Hut 8 operated at a profit.

**Revenue Mix:** The \$71M was dominated by the **Compute** segment (AI data center hosting), which grew from negligible levels a year ago. Bitcoin mining revenue was flat-to-down due to the April 2024 halving and network difficulty increases.

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity

- **Cash:** Limited after deploying capital into AI infrastructure
- **Debt:** Recently raised \$4.25B in investment-grade construction bonds for Beacon Point
- **Bitcoin Treasury:** ~10,278 BTC held on balance sheet (~\$1.1B at \$107K/BTC)
- **Total Contracted Revenue:** \$16.8B across AI leases (base term)

### Cash Flow

Operating cash flow is negative due to heavy capex on AI data center construction. The company is in a classic infrastructure build-out phase: burning cash today to generate contracted revenue for 15+ years. The \$9.8B base-term lease at Beacon Point (15-year triple-net) provides revenue visibility that most miners lack.

## 3. Valuation

Hut 8 trades at a significant premium to pure-play bitcoin miners, reflecting its AI pivot. However, the valuation is highly sensitive to:
1. Bitcoin price (treasury mark-to-market and mining cash flow)
2. AI lease execution (construction timelines, tenant ramp)
3. Interest rates (cost of project finance)

| Scenario | Price Target | Assumptions |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Bull** | \$180 – \$220 | AI leases execute on schedule; BTC > \$120K; additional tenant signings at 8,375 MW pipeline; successful refinancing of mining ops |
| **Base** | \$110 – \$145 | Beacon Point delivers first revenue 2027; BTC \$90–110K; mining cash flow stable; debt service coverage > 1.2x |
| **Bear** | \$45 – \$65 | Construction delays; tenant default or renegotiation; BTC < \$70K; mining becomes unprofitable; liquidity crunch |

**Valuation Multiples:**
- EV/Sales: ~36x (trailing) — expensive vs. miners, cheap vs. AI infrastructure REITs
- P/B: Elevated due to BTC treasury mark-to-market volatility
- The "right" comp is a blend: 50% bitcoin miner, 50% data center REIT. Neither comp fits perfectly.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **Beacon Point Ramp (2026–2027):** 352 MW initial lease with an investment-grade tenant. First revenue expected late 2026/early 2027. This is the proof point for the AI thesis.

2. **River Bend Financing Closed:** \$3.25B bond offering (April 2026) and \$4.25B additional notes (May 2026) provide construction capital. The 16.5-year fully amortizing structure eliminates refinancing risk post-completion.

3. **8,375 MW Pipeline:** Beyond contracted capacity, Hut 8 has a multi-gigawatt development pipeline. Each additional gigawatt signed = ~\$20B+ in potential contract value.

4. **Bitcoin Halving Efficiency:** Hut 8 has upgraded to latest-generation ASICs (Bitmain S21 / MicroBT M60 series). If BTC price holds above \$80K, mining remains cash-flow positive despite halved block rewards.

5. **Institutional Board Upgrade:** E. Stanley O'Neal's appointment as chair improves governance credibility and may attract ESG-focused institutional capital.

## 5. Risk Factors

**Business Risks:**
- **Execution Risk:** Hut 8 has never built and operated AI data centers at this scale. Delays, cost overruns, or operational failures at Beacon Point would crater the thesis.
- **Tenant Concentration:** The \$9.8B lease is with a single (undisclosed) investment-grade tenant. Any default or renegotiation would be catastrophic.
- **Bitcoin Dependency:** Despite the AI pivot, ~40–50% of near-term cash flow still comes from mining. A BTC crash below \$60K would strain liquidity.

**Financial Risks:**
- **Leverage:** \$7B+ in project debt for AI campuses. If revenue ramp is delayed, debt service becomes unsustainable.
- **BTC Volatility:** Quarterly earnings will swing wildly based on mark-to-market accounting for the 10,278 BTC treasury. This creates headline risk regardless of operations.
- **Dilution:** If construction costs exceed budgets or revenue ramps slower than expected, equity raises are likely.

**Macro/Sector Risks:**
- **AI Demand Cyclicality:** The current AI infrastructure boom may cool. If GPU demand normalizes or shifts to edge inference, hyperscale data center demand could soften.
- **Power Market Risk:** Hut 8's economics depend on long-term power contracts. If Texas power prices spike (e.g., 2021-style freeze), margins compress.
- **Regulatory:** Crypto mining faces ESG scrutiny. AI data centers face local permitting and grid interconnection delays.

## 6. Recommendation

**Rating: SPEC. BUY**

Hut 8 is a high-conviction, high-risk transition story. The AI data center pivot is not hype — it is backed by \$16.8B in contracted revenue and investment-grade project finance. However, the company is still a bitcoin miner at its core, and the AI revenue is 12–18 months away from meaningful contribution.

**Position Sizing:** 2–3% of a speculative/growth portfolio. Not a core holding.

## 7. Readability Pass

- **Bitcoin Halving:** Every four years, the reward for mining a bitcoin block is cut in half. The April 2024 halving reduced miner revenue by 50% overnight, forcing inefficient miners to shut down.
- **Triple-Net Lease:** The tenant pays all property expenses (taxes, insurance, maintenance) in addition to rent. Hut 8 gets a "clean" rent check with minimal operating responsibility.
- **Mark-to-Market Accounting:** Hut 8 must revalue its bitcoin holdings every quarter based on the current BTC price. This creates paper gains/losses that hit the income statement even if no bitcoin was sold.
- **MW (Megawatt):** A unit of power capacity. 1 MW = roughly 1,000 homes. Hut 8's 8,375 MW pipeline is enough to power a small city.
- **Investment-Grade Bond:** Debt rated BBB- or higher by rating agencies. Hut 8's ability to issue this debt validates the credit quality of its AI lease contracts.
