# PESI — Perma-Fix Environmental Services, Inc.

Perma-Fix Environmental Services is a U.S. environmental-services company specializing in the treatment, processing, and disposal of nuclear, low-level radioactive, mixed, hazardous, and non-hazardous waste. The Treatment segment (~73% of FY2025 revenue) is anchored by the Perma-Fix Northwest (PFNW) facility adjacent to the Hanford DOE site, while the Services segment (~27%) provides on-site waste management, radiological support, demolition, and PFAS treatment. The bull case is that a Hanford Direct-Feed Low-Activity Waste (DFLAW) / Effluent Treatment Facility (ETF) ramp and a potential large-scale grouting opportunity materially expand throughput at PFNW, transforming a loss-making business into a profitable DOE nuclear-cleanup contractor. The bear case is that federal procurement remains slow, the going-concern warning turns into a liquidity crunch, and the \$20M equity raise dilutes shareholders without a commensurate revenue inflection.

## Company Overview

Perma-Fix Environmental Services, Inc. (NASDAQ: PESI) operates through two segments: **Treatment** and **Services**.

**Treatment Segment** (~73% of 2025 revenue, ~\$45.1M, up 29% YoY)
- Four licensed U.S. treatment/processing/disposal facilities: Perma-Fix Northwest (PFNW) in Richland, Washington; Perma-Fix Florida; East Tennessee; and Diversified Scientific Services (DSSI) in Tennessee/Kentucky.
- PFNW is the strategic crown jewel: located near the Hanford DOE reservation, it is the only commercial facility in the region capable of treating a broad range of radioactive and mixed wastes. The facility’s permit and infrastructure position it to capture Hanford tank-waste treatment and ETF/DFLAW effluent streams.
- The segment processes nuclear, low-level radioactive, mixed, hazardous, and non-hazardous waste, typically under long-term contracts or task orders with DOE contractors, government agencies, utilities, and research institutions.

**Services Segment** (~27% of 2025 revenue, ~\$16.6M, down 31% YoY)
- On-site waste management, technical and radiological services, health physics, demolition/disposal, and PFAS treatment.
- The segment is project-based and more sensitive to federal budget timing, procurement delays, and facility-specific milestones. The 2025 decline reflects slower project starts and a revenue-recognition lumpiness typical of government contracting.

**Customers and End-Market**
- The U.S. Department of Energy and its prime contractors dominate revenue. Other customers include federal/state agencies, commercial utilities, research institutions, and industrial generators of hazardous waste.
- Revenue is therefore tied to DOE environmental-management appropriations, Hanford lifecycle spending, and broader federal infrastructure for nuclear remediation.

**Competitive Position**
- Perma-Fix is one of the few commercial, licensed U.S. handlers of radioactive and mixed waste. The permits, regulatory relationships, and embedded infrastructure near Hanford create meaningful barriers to entry.
- However, the business is capital intensive, low-margin, and heavily dependent on a single customer ecosystem, which limits pricing power and creates lumpy revenue.

**Management and Governance**
- The company is led by a long-tenured management team with deep experience in nuclear-waste logistics, DOE contracting, and environmental permitting. Insider ownership is meaningful, aligning management with the equity story, though the recent equity raise diluted existing holders.

## Financial Analysis

**Income Statement (FY2025 and Q1 2026)**
- FY2025 revenue: \$61.67M, up 4.3% YoY.
  - Treatment revenue: \$45.1M (+29%).
  - Services revenue: \$16.6M (-31%).
- Gross profit: \$6.0M in FY2025, implying a thin gross margin of roughly 9.7%.
- Operating loss: \$11.7M.
- EBITDA from continuing operations: ~\$9.7M (management-adjusted; note the sign convention relative to reported operating loss).
- Net loss: \$13.78M.
- Diluted EPS: \$0.75.
- Q1 2026 revenue: \$11.1M (-20% YoY), gross loss \$2.9M, operating loss \$7.5M, net loss \$7.5M, EPS \$0.40.

**Balance Sheet and Liquidity**
- Cash and short-term investments: ~\$6.7M as of Q1 2026.
- Total debt: ~\$4.1M.
- Stockholders’ equity: ~\$43.0M.
- The Q1 2026 10-Q includes a **going-concern warning**, citing doubt about the company’s ability to continue as a going concern absent improved operating results, additional financing, or both.
- In May 2026 the company raised \$20M of equity at \$8.75/share, extending liquidity but diluting existing shareholders.

**Cash Flow**
- Trailing-twelve-month free cash flow is approximately negative \$17.6M, driven by operating losses, working capital absorption, and capex tied to facility readiness.
- The business is currently cash-consumptive. A return to positive free cash flow depends on materially higher throughput at PFNW and a rebound in Services billings.

**Segment Trends**
- Treatment is the growth driver and the margin lever. Services revenue is cyclical and should be modeled as a recovery contributor rather than the core thesis.
- The Q1 2026 decline is not necessarily structural; management has guided to improved performance from Q2 2026 onward, driven by Hanford receipts and the Lawrence Livermore demolition contract.

## Valuation

PESI is a micro-cap turnaround with a speculative, asset-driven bull case. Valuation is best framed as a scenario analysis rather than a precise DCF, because the variance around future Hanford/DOE volumes is large and the current income statement is loss-making.

**Current Metrics**
- Price (2026-07-17): \$17.80.
- Market cap: ~\$383M.
- 52-week range: \$8.19–\$17.80.
- Stock is at 52-week highs after a roughly 16% intraday move on 2026-07-17 on heavy volume, likely tied to momentum around the Hanford/grouting narrative.

**Comparable Considerations**
- There are few direct publicly traded comparables. Government-services and nuclear-remediation contractors trade on backlog, contract visibility, and EV/Sales multiples. PESI currently trades at a premium to its recent revenue run-rate on the expectation of a step-change in Hanford-derived revenue.
- The \$216M/year speculative grouting opportunity cited in some sell-side/retail analysis would, if real and awarded, transform the revenue base by roughly 3.5x. We do not bake that in at full value, but it explains why the market cap can exceed the current revenue base by a wide margin.

**Scenario Targets**
- **Bull case:** \$26–\$30. Assumes the Hanford ETF/DFLAW ramp accelerates in H2 2026, PFNW reaches materially higher utilization, the Lawrence Livermore contract ramps as planned, and Perma-FIX captures a meaningful share of a grouting opportunity. Margins expand on operating leverage, and the company returns to profitability by 2027.
- **Base case:** \$18–\$20. Assumes a modest Hanford improvement and a gradual Services recovery, but no transformational grouting contract. Losses narrow through 2026–2027 and the company stabilizes at a smaller, still-cash-constrained scale. The current price roughly embeds the base case.
- **Bear case:** \$10–\$12. Assumes DOE procurement delays persist, the going-concern warning forces further dilutive financing, and the Hanford ramp disappoints. The stock would re-test the post-raise lows and likely trade near tangible-book value.

**Discounted Cash Flow Sensibility Check**
- A DCF is not reliable given the binary path to profitability and the dependence on uncertain government awards. We therefore anchor price targets to revenue-multiple and book-value ranges implied by the scenario outcomes above.

## Growth Catalysts

1. **Hanford ETF/DFLAW Waste Receipts at PFNW**
   - The Department of Energy’s Direct-Feed Low-Activity Waste program is intended to immobilize and dispose of millions of gallons of legacy tank waste. PFNW is positioned to receive and treat associated waste streams. Management has flagged a Q2 2026 and H2 2026 ramp in receipts as the primary driver of improved financial performance.

2. **Speculative Grouting Opportunity**
   - A Seeking Alpha–referenced narrative suggests a potential \$216M/year grouting opportunity at Hanford. We treat this as unconfirmed and high-risk, but it is clearly part of the current stock momentum. If a contract of anywhere near that size is awarded, the bull case would be materially underwritten.

3. **Lawrence Livermore Demolition/Disposal Contract**
   - In March 2026 Perma-Fix announced a two-year, \$24M contract with Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory for demolition and disposal services. This provides near-term Services revenue and validates the company’s on-site capabilities.

4. **Richland Permit Expansion**
   - In January 2026 the company expanded its Richland permit, tripling liquid mixed-waste treatment capacity. This increases the theoretical throughput at PFNW ahead of the expected Hanford ramp.

5. **Perma-FAS PFAS Destruction Technology**
   - Perma-FAS is the company’s proprietary PFAS destruction technology. PFAS remediation is a secular growth market driven by EPA action and litigation, and a commercial-scale deployment would diversify the revenue base beyond nuclear waste.

6. **Q2 2026 Earnings**
   - Scheduled for around August 6, 2026. This will be the first clean read on whether the Hanford ramp and the Lawrence Livermore contract are translating into revenue and margin improvement. A beat or raised guidance would likely drive the next leg of the stock.

## Risk Factors

**Business / Operational Risks**
- **Customer concentration:** The DOE and its contractors account for the majority of revenue. Any delay, cancellation, or re-bidding of Hanford-related work would have an outsized impact.
- **Permit and regulatory risk:** Treatment and disposal operations require licenses, permits, and environmental compliance. Violations or permit delays could halt operations.
- **Project execution:** On-site demolition, waste packaging, and transportation are operationally complex and can generate one-time charges or safety incidents.

**Financial Risks**
- **Going-concern warning:** The Q1 2026 10-Q explicitly raises substantial doubt about the company’s ability to continue as a going concern. Without a revenue inflection or further financing, liquidity could become critical.
- **Dilution:** The May 2026 \$20M equity raise at \$8.75 was highly dilutive from the current price. Further raises are possible if the Hanford ramp is delayed.
- **Low margin structure:** Historical gross margins are thin; the company must achieve high operating leverage to reach sustained profitability.

**Macro / Sector Risks**
- **Federal budget and procurement cycles:** DOE environmental-management budgets are subject to appropriations, continuing resolutions, and administration priorities. A slowdown in federal spending would directly hit revenue.
- **Competition and re-contracting:** Larger DOE primes and regional competitors can bid for on-site and treatment work, pressuring margins and market share.
- **Speculative narrative risk:** The grouting opportunity is unconfirmed. If it fails to materialize, the stock could re-rate sharply lower.

## Recommendation

**Rating: SPEC. BUY**

We rate PESI a **SPEC. BUY** with a **base-case target of \$18–\$20** and a **bull case of \$26–\$30**. The thesis is a near-term operational inflection at the PFNW facility driven by Hanford ETF/DFLAW receipts and a broader DOE cleanup cycle. The stock has already run hard, but the potential revenue step-change and tight share structure could drive further upside if Q2 2026 results confirm the ramp.

**Position Sizing and Entry Strategy**
- This is a high-conviction, high-risk speculative position. Allocate only what you can afford to lose completely.
- Consider scaling in around the current level or on any pullback toward \$15–\$16. Avoid chasing large intraday spikes unless Q2 2026 results or a contract announcement provides fundamental confirmation.
- For active traders, a break above \$18 on volume would confirm the bullish momentum, while a failure to hold \$15 would signal a deeper consolidation.

**Stop Loss / Risk Management**
- A hard stop near \$13.50–\$14.00 (below the recent post-raise consolidation zone) protects against a fundamental disappointment or liquidity scare.
- Reduce size if the Q2 2026 report shows no sequential improvement in Treatment revenue or if management walks back the Hanford ramp guidance.

**Catalyst Calendar**
- **August 2026:** Q2 2026 earnings — the key near-term catalyst.
- **H2 2026:** Hanford ETF/DFLAW waste receipt ramp at PFNW.
- **2026–2027:** Lawrence Livermore contract execution; any grouting or PFAS commercial news.

## Readability Pass

Perma-Fix cleans up dangerous waste, mostly for the U.S. Department of Energy. Its most important facility is near the Hanford nuclear site in Washington State, where decades of Cold War tank waste need to be treated and disposed of. The company has been losing money, but management believes business will pick up in the second half of 2026 as more Hanford waste arrives. If that happens — and especially if Perma-Fix wins a large grouting contract — the stock could move to \$26–\$30. If the ramp is delayed or the company runs low on cash, the stock could fall back to \$10–\$12. We rate it SPEC. BUY because the upside is meaningful but the risks are real.
