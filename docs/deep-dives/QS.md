## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

QuantumScape is a pre-revenue solid-state battery developer founded in 2010 and headquartered in San Jose, California. The company went public via SPAC merger in November 2020 and is backed by Volkswagen Group, one of the largest automotive OEMs globally.

**Business Model:** Asset-light licensing and separator supply. QuantumScape does not intend to build gigafactories. Instead, it plans to license its proprietary ceramic separator technology and cell designs to OEM partners, while supplying the critical separator material itself. This avoids the multi-billion-dollar capital intensity of full cell manufacturing.

**Technology:** The core innovation is a lithium-metal anode paired with a proprietary solid ceramic separator. This architecture promises:
- **Energy density:** 400–500 Wh/kg (vs. ~250–300 for conventional Li-ion)
- **Faster charging:** 10–80% in under 15 minutes
- **Improved safety:** Non-flammable solid electrolyte eliminates thermal runaway risk
- **Longer cycle life:** Targeting 1,000+ cycles with minimal degradation

**Key Partner:** Volkswagen has invested over \$300M, collaborates on joint development, and holds board representation. Additional OEM relationships (un disclosed) are in various stages of evaluation.

**Management:** CEO Jagdeep Singh co-founded the company and has steered it through SPAC merger and multiple funding rounds. The team includes veterans from Tesla, Apple, and major battery manufacturers.

## 10. READABILITY & CLARITY PASS

**What is a solid-state battery?**  
Conventional EV batteries use a liquid electrolyte — the gooey substance that lets lithium ions flow between the anode and cathode. Solid-state batteries replace this liquid with a solid ceramic or glass material. Think of it like replacing a water balloon with a ceramic tile: it cannot leak, catch fire, or degrade in the same way.

**Why does energy density matter?**  
Energy density measures how much energy a battery can store per unit of weight. A 400–500 Wh/kg solid-state battery could give an EV 500+ miles of range with the same battery pack size as today’s 300-mile cars. More range, less weight.

**What is a ceramic separator?**  
The separator is the critical barrier inside the battery that keeps the positive and negative sides from touching (which would cause a short circuit). QuantumScape’s innovation is a solid ceramic separator that also acts as the electrolyte, enabling a lithium-metal anode that stores far more energy than conventional graphite anodes.

**What does "asset-light licensing" mean?**  
Instead of building massive factories (like Tesla’s Gigafactories), QuantumScape plans to let car companies build the batteries using QuantumScape’s designs, while QuantumScape sells them the critical ceramic separator material. This reduces capital needs but means revenue depends on partners actually building the batteries.

**Why is cash runway important?**  
QuantumScape spends ~\$300M per year and has ~\$1B cash. Simple math: \$1B ÷ \$300M/year = ~3.3 years. If they cannot start generating revenue by then, they must sell more shares to raise money — diluting existing shareholders.
