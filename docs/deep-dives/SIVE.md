## 1. Company Overview

**Sivers Semiconductors AB (publ)** is a Swedish semiconductor company founded in 1951 and headquartered in Kista, Sweden. The company develops, manufactures, and sells chips, components, modules, and subsystems based on proprietary semiconductor technology in the microwave, millimeter wave, and optical semiconductor domains.

The business operates through two primary segments:

- **Wireless Communication:** MMICs (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuits), RF modules, and antenna solutions for 5G, SATCOM, and radar applications. Key products include beamforming chips for phased-array antennas used in satellite ground terminals and 5G base stations.
- **Photonics:** Optical semiconductor components for fiber-optic communications and sensing, including lasers, photodetectors, and integrated optical subsystems.

**Competitive Moat:** Sivers holds proprietary III-V semiconductor process technology (InP, GaAs) that is difficult to replicate. The company is a key supplier to the rapidly growing low-earth orbit (LEO) satellite terminal market, with partnerships spanning major satellite operators and telecom infrastructure vendors. The dual focus on wireless and photonics creates cross-selling opportunities in integrated RF/optical systems for next-generation networks.

**Management:** CEO Anders Storm leads a team with deep semiconductor industry experience. The company has undergone strategic repositioning toward higher-margin custom ASIC and module solutions, moving away from commoditized component sales.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **LEO Satellite Terminal Boom:** The global LEO satellite constellation market (Starlink, OneWeb, Amazon Kuiper, Telesat Lightspeed) is driving demand for phased-array ground terminals. Sivers' beamforming chips are designed into multiple terminal programs. As constellation deployments accelerate in 2026–2027, chip volumes could scale rapidly.

2. **5G mmWave Infrastructure:** Sivers' mmWave products target 5G base station and backhaul applications. While this market has been slower to develop than expected, ongoing 5G rollouts in Asia and North America represent incremental revenue opportunity.

3. **Dual-Listing in the United States:** The company has publicly stated its intention to pursue a dual listing on a U.S. exchange (likely NASDAQ). A U.S. listing would expand the investor base, improve liquidity, and potentially re-rate the stock toward U.S. semiconductor multiples. The 2025 annual report was adjusted to align with U.S. GAAP/SEC reporting standards in preparation.

4. **Photonics Division Recovery:** The optical communications market is recovering from the 2023–2024 inventory correction. Sivers' photonics products (lasers, detectors) for data center and telecom fiber optics could see renewed demand as hyperscalers expand optical interconnects for AI clusters.

5. **Strategic Partnerships / M&A Optionality:** Sivers' proprietary III-V technology and satellite terminal design wins make it an attractive acquisition target for larger RF/semiconductor companies (Qorvo, MACOM, Analog Devices) seeking mmWave/satellite capabilities.

## 5. Risk Factors

1. **Customer Concentration:** A significant portion of revenue is tied to a small number of satellite terminal customers. Loss of a major program or delayed orders would materially impact revenue.

2. **Pre-Profitability / Cash Burn:** Sivers is not yet profitable and relies on cash reserves to fund operations. Extended delays in revenue growth could necessitate additional capital raises, diluting existing shareholders.

3. **FX Exposure:** Revenue is primarily USD-denominated (satellite customers) while costs are largely SEK-based. SEK/USD volatility impacts reported revenue and margins.

4. **5G mmWave Market Timing:** The mmWave 5G market has underperformed expectations for years. If 5G infrastructure spending remains focused on sub-6 GHz, Sivers' mmWave revenue opportunity may be smaller than anticipated.

5. **Competition:** Large RF semiconductor companies (Qorvo, Skyworks, MACOM) have greater resources and could develop competing beamforming solutions. Sivers must maintain its technology lead and customer relationships to defend market position.

6. **Dual-Listing Execution Risk:** The U.S. listing process is complex, time-consuming, and uncertain. Delays or failure to complete the listing would remove a key catalyst.

## 7. Readability Pass

- **LEO (Low Earth Orbit):** Satellites orbiting 200–2,000 km above Earth. LEO constellations (like Starlink) require thousands of small satellites and ground terminals to provide global internet coverage.
- **Phased-Array Antenna:** An antenna system that uses multiple small elements and electronic beam steering (no moving parts) to track satellites across the sky. Sivers' chips control the beam direction.
- **MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit):** A chip that integrates multiple microwave functions (amplification, switching, phase shifting) on a single semiconductor die. Essential for compact, low-cost satellite terminals.
- **III-V Semiconductors:** A class of semiconductor materials (Indium Phosphide, Gallium Arsenide) that operate at higher frequencies than silicon, making them ideal for mmWave and optical applications.
- **Beamforming:** A signal processing technique that focuses radio energy in a specific direction rather than broadcasting in all directions. Critical for satellite terminals to maintain a strong link to fast-moving LEO satellites.
