## Executive Summary

Circle Internet Group (NYSE: CRCL) is the issuer of USDC, the world's second-largest stablecoin with a market cap of approximately $75 billion and 25% market share. The company completed its highly successful NYSE IPO in June 2025 at $31/share, with the stock experiencing explosive growth to a peak of ~$299 before settling around $94-96 in April 2026. Circle generates nearly 100% of its revenue from interest income on USDC reserves held primarily in short-term U.S. Treasuries through BlackRock's Circle Reserve Fund. The company represents a pure-play investment on the growth of regulated digital dollars and the emerging stablecoin economy.

## 1. Company Overview

### 1.1 Business Model

Circle Internet Group operates as a financial technology company that issues USDC (USD Coin), a fiat-collateralized stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the U.S. dollar. The company's core value proposition is enabling businesses and individuals to use digital dollars on blockchain networks for payments, commerce, and financial applications worldwide.

**Primary Revenue Stream:**
Circle earns nearly 100% of its revenue from interest income on USDC reserves. When users purchase USDC, Circle holds the corresponding U.S. dollars in reserve and invests them in short-term U.S. Treasury securities and cash equivalents. The interest earned on these reserves—totaling $1.68 billion in 2024 and $2.75 billion in 2025—represents Circle's gross revenue.

**How It Works:**
1. Users deposit U.S. dollars with Circle or partners
2. Circle mints equivalent USDC tokens on blockchain networks
3. Circle invests the dollar deposits in the Circle Reserve Fund (managed by BlackRock)
4. The fund holds short-term U.S. Treasuries, Treasury repos, and cash
5. Interest income flows to Circle as revenue
6. Users can redeem USDC 1:1 for dollars at any time

## 4. Growth Catalysts

### 4.1 Regulatory Tailwinds

**GENIUS Act (U.S.):**
- Passed by Senate June 2025, House pending
- Creates clear regulatory framework for stablecoin issuers
- Requires 1:1 reserves, monthly attestations, redemption rights
- Effectively bans unregulated competitors like current USDT structure
- Expected to drive institutional adoption of regulated stablecoins
- **Impact:** Significant positive for USDC as the most regulated major stablecoin

**MiCA (EU):**
- Markets in Crypto-Assets regulation fully implemented 2024-2025
- Circle received French EMI license; EURC and USDC MiCA-compliant
- Tether refused to comply; USDT delisted from EU exchanges
- **Impact:** Competitive moat in world's second-largest economy

**Global Regulatory Trend:**
- UK, Singapore, UAE, Japan developing stablecoin frameworks
- Circle already licensed in 46 U.S. states + DC + Puerto Rico
- Compliance infrastructure creates barrier to entry
- **Impact:** First-mover advantage in regulated markets

- Single product dependency (USDC)
- No meaningful revenue diversification
- **Mitigation:** New products (USYC, Payments Network) still nascent

**2. Coinbase Dependence:**
- Coinbase captures 50-56% of USDC reserve revenue
- Revenue sharing terms locked in
- Coinbase's USDC share growing (22% in Q1 2025)
- **Impact:** Limits Circle's ability to capture full economics

## 11. Readability & Clarity Pass: Jargon Translation

### What Circle Actually Does (Simple Version)

**Circle is basically a digital bank that issues "digital dollars" called USDC.**

Here's how it works in plain English:
1. You give Circle $100 real dollars
2. Circle gives you 100 USDC digital tokens
3. Circle takes your $100 and buys safe government bonds
4. The government pays Circle interest on those bonds
5. That interest is Circle's profit
6. You can trade your USDC or cash it back out anytime

**The key insight:** Circle makes money from the interest on all the dollars people have deposited with them. The more people use USDC, the more money Circle makes.

### Key Terms Explained

| Jargon | Simple Meaning |
|--------|----------------|
| **Stablecoin** | A cryptocurrency that stays at $1 (stable value) |
| **Reserve** | The real money Circle holds to back every USDC token |
| **Market Cap** | Total value of all USDC in circulation (price × quantity) |
| **DeFi** | "Decentralized Finance" - banking without traditional banks, using code |
| **Treasury Yield** | Interest rate the government pays on its short-term loans |
| **P/S Ratio** | Stock price compared to company sales (higher = more expensive) |
| **Revenue Sharing** | Splitting profits with partners (Circle shares with Coinbase) |
| **Attestation** | An auditor's report saying "yes, Circle really has the money" |
| **MiCA** | New European crypto rules that favor regulated companies like Circle |
| **GENIUS Act** | Proposed U.S. law to regulate stablecoins (good for Circle) |
| **CCTP** | Software that lets USDC move between different blockchains |

### The Interest Rate Connection (Why This Matters)

Circle's profits depend heavily on interest rates. Here's why:

- **High rates (like 2023-2024):** Government bonds pay 4-5% interest → Circle makes lots of money
- **Low rates (like 2020-2021):** Government bonds pay 0-1% interest → Circle makes very little

**Think of it like this:** If you had $1 billion in a savings account, you'd rather earn 5% ($50M/year) than 1% ($10M/year).

### The Coinbase Relationship Explained

Coinbase is like Circle's biggest salesperson. They help people buy USDC. In exchange:
- Coinbase gets 50% of the interest on USDC held on their platform
- This is why Coinbase "rewards" users with interest on USDC - they're sharing the revenue

**The risk:** Coinbase is becoming so big (22% of all USDC) that they're taking a huge chunk of Circle's profits.

### Competition in Plain English

| Competitor | The Simple Story |
|------------|------------------|
| **Tether (USDT)** | The big kid on the block. 3x bigger than USDC, but less transparent. Some worry about whether they really have all the money they claim. |
| **Ethena (USDe)** | New competitor that actually pays you interest directly. Growing fast but riskier. |
| **Banks** | Traditional banks want to issue their own digital dollars. Could be serious competition if they get regulatory approval. |

### Why the Stock Price is So Volatile

Circle's stock swings wildly because:
1. **It's new** - IPO was June 2025, so price discovery ongoing
2. **Rates change** - Every Fed meeting moves the stock
3. **Crypto correlation** - Even though it's "stable," it's still a crypto stock
4. **High expectations** - Investors expect massive growth

### The Bottom Line for Investors

**Bull Case:** If digital dollars become a big deal (like $1+ trillion market), Circle could be worth 5-10x more.

**Bear Case:** If interest rates crash or banks take over, Circle could be worth half or less.

**Key Question:** Do you believe digital dollars on blockchains will become a major part of global finance? If yes, Circle is a pure-play way to bet on that. If no, stay away.
