# NB \u2014 NioCorp Developments Ltd.

NioCorp Developments is advancing the Elk Creek Critical Minerals Project in southeastern Nebraska \u2014 the only advanced niobium / scandium / titanium project in North America and home to the 2nd largest indicated rare earth resource in the United States. With a feasibility study confirming a $2.8 billion NPV, 29.2% IRR, and 38-year mine life, NioCorp is positioned to become a strategic domestic supplier of minerals critical to defense, aerospace, electric vehicles, and clean energy. The company broke ground on the $44.6M mine portal in March 2026, has raised over $370M in funding, and signed a rare earth offtake term sheet with Stellantis. The stock has fallen 66% from its 52-week high of $12.58, creating an attractive entry for patient investors willing to hold through the multi-year construction phase.

## Company Overview

### The Elk Creek Critical Minerals Project

The Elk Creek Project is located approximately 65 miles southeast of Lincoln, Nebraska, and represents one of the most strategically important critical minerals deposits in the United States. The project contains:

- **Niobium**: Highest-grade niobium resource in North America. Niobium is essential for high-strength, low-alloy steel used in infrastructure, automotive, and defense applications. It is also a key input for solid-state lithium-ion batteries.
- **Scandium**: Once in production, the project would make the U.S. a major prospective producer of scandium, which strengthens aluminum alloys for aerospace and automotive applications.
- **Titanium**: A refractory metal used across aerospace, defense, medical, and industrial applications. The U.S. is heavily reliant on foreign imports, including historically from Russia and Ukraine.
- **Rare Earth Elements**: The 2nd largest indicated rare earth resource in the U.S., including neodymium-praseodymium (NdPr), dysprosium, and terbium \u2014 all critical for permanent magnets in EVs and wind turbines.

- Debt: EXIM Bank loan, project finance facilities
- Equity: Strategic investments (Stellantis), follow-on equity raises
- Government grants: Additional DoD, DOE, or DFC funding

The company\u2019s ability to secure non-dilutive or minimally dilutive financing will be a key determinant of shareholder returns.

## Growth Catalysts

### US Critical Minerals Security Agenda

The Elk Creek Project aligns directly with U.S. national security and clean energy priorities. The U.S. is 100% import-dependent on niobium and scandium and heavily reliant on China for rare earth processing. NioCorp\u2019s domestic production would:
- Reduce dependence on foreign supply chains for defense-critical materials
- Support the EV transition with domestic rare earth magnet supply
- Enable lighter, stronger aluminum-scandium alloys for aerospace and automotive
- Create hundreds of high-paying jobs in rural Nebraska

This strategic alignment increases the probability of continued government support through grants, loans, and offtake agreements.

### Stellantis offtake and Strategic Investment

The Stellantis term sheet is a significant validation of the project\u2019s commercial viability. Stellantis needs secure, non-Chinese rare earth supply for its EV platforms (Jeep, Ram, Peugeot, Fiat). A definitive agreement would:
- Provide revenue visibility for the rare earth stream
- Potentially include a strategic equity investment at a premium to market
- Open doors to additional automotive OEM partnerships

### Project Financing Close

The next 12-18 months will determine whether Elk Creek moves from development to construction. Key financing milestones:
- EXIM Bank loan decision
- Potential DFC or DOE loan guarantees
- Stellantis strategic investment
- Additional offtake agreements (defense, aerospace)
- Possible streaming/royalty transaction on niobium or titanium

If NioCorp can secure 60-70% of project capex through debt and strategic equity, the dilution to existing shareholders will be manageable.

### First Production and Revenue

Assuming construction begins in 2026-2027, first production of niobium, scandium, and titanium is targeted for 2028-2029. Rare earth production may follow in a second phase. The long mine life (38 years) and low operating costs (per feasibility study) suggest strong free cash flow generation once at steady-state production.

## Risk Factors

### Financing and Dilution Risk

The project requires ~$1.1B in capex. While NioCorp has $419M in cash, additional financing is required. If debt is unavailable or expensive, the company may need to raise significant equity, diluting existing shareholders. The stock\u2019s 66% decline from highs reflects market concerns about this financing gap.

### Construction and Execution Risk

Elk Creek is a complex, multi-product mining and processing facility. Construction delays, cost overruns, or technical issues in the processing plant could derail the project economics. The demonstration plant has de-risked the flow sheet, but scaling to commercial production is a major step.

### Commodity Price Risk

NioCorp\u2019s revenue will depend on niobium, scandium, titanium, and rare earth prices. While these commodities are strategically important and supply-constrained, price volatility is significant. A sustained downturn in Chinese rare earth prices or niobium demand could compress project margins.

### Competition

- **MP Materials (MP)**: Already producing rare earths at Mountain Pass, California; building US separation facility
- **Lynas Rare Earths**: Australian producer with US processing facility under development
- **Energy Fuels (UUUU)**: Producing separated heavy rare earths at White Mesa Mill
- **Other niobium producers**: CBMM (Brazil) dominates global niobium supply; NioCorp would be a niche player

### Regulatory and Environmental Risk

Mining projects in the US face intense regulatory scrutiny. While NioCorp has secured key permits and is aligned with the Equator Principles, environmental opposition or permitting delays could impact the construction timeline.

### Pre-Revenue Status

NioCorp is years away from first revenue. The stock will trade on sentiment, financing news, and construction progress rather than fundamentals. This creates volatility and requires a multi-year investment horizon.

## Readability Pass

**What NioCorp actually does:** Imagine a hole in the ground in Nebraska that contains a unique mix of minerals the US currently imports almost entirely from other countries \u2014 mainly China and Brazil. NioCorp is building a mine to extract these minerals: niobium (makes steel stronger and lighter), scandium (makes aluminum alloys weldable and corrosion-resistant), titanium (used in jets and medical implants), and rare earths (essential for EV motors and wind turbines). It\u2019s like a strategic gas station for American industry \u2014 except the gas is rocks.

**What the financials mean:** NioCorp has $419M in the bank and is spending about $28M per year on development. It makes no revenue yet \u2014 the mine won\u2019t produce anything until 2028-2029. The company loses about $51M per year, which is normal for a pre-production miner. The key number is the $2.8 billion NPV: if the mine gets built and operates as planned, it\u2019s worth far more than the current $626M market cap. But that\u2019s a big if.

**What the risk/reward looks like:** If everything goes right \u2014 financing closes, Stellantis invests, construction stays on budget \u2014 the stock could reach $10\u2013$12 (150\u2013200% upside). If things go sideways, it probably trades between $5\u2013$8 as construction progresses. If financing fails or the project stalls, it could fall to $2\u2013$3. At $4.30, you\u2019re paying for a distressed pre-production asset with world-class geology. The downside is real, but the upside is substantial if the team executes.
