# NB — NioCorp Developments Ltd.

NioCorp Developments is a pre-revenue critical minerals developer advancing the Elk Creek Project in southeast Nebraska, a large underground deposit of niobium, scandium, titanium, and rare earth elements. The project is positioned as a domestic source of materials used in high-strength steel, aerospace alloys, EV batteries, and defense applications. With a non-binding 10-year offtake agreement with Traxys announced in mid-2026 and ongoing project-financing efforts, the stock trades as a binary, catalyst-driven development play.

## 1. Company Overview

NioCorp Developments Ltd. (NASDAQ: NB) is a Canadian-headquartered development-stage mining company. Its sole focus is the Elk Creek Critical Minerals Project in Nebraska, which contains indicated and inferred resources of niobium, scandium, titanium, and rare earth oxides. The company is pre-revenue and is working to complete project financing, finalize construction contracts, and secure additional offtake agreements before breaking ground.

### Business Model

NioCorp is not yet a producer. Its value is derived from the development, construction, and eventual operation of Elk Creek. The planned product mix includes:

- **Niobium** — used in high-strength, low-alloy steel and superalloys for aerospace and defense.
- **Scandium** — strengthens aluminum alloys; used in aerospace, EVs, and solid oxide fuel cells.
- **Titanium** — feedstock for pigments and titanium metal; the project has demonstrated improved recovery rates.
- **Rare Earth Elements** — the 2022 feasibility study highlighted Elk Creek as the second-largest indicated-or-better rare earth resource in the U.S.

The company is pursuing a vertically integrated model where it captures value through mine production, downstream processing, and potential partnerships with U.S. and allied manufacturers.

### Management & Strategy

The management team has focused on de-risking the project through:

- U.S. government engagement, including the Export-Import Bank of the United States (EXIM) for potential debt financing.
- Land acquisition, which the company completed in 2025, clearing the site for construction.
- A non-binding 10-year offtake framework with Traxys North America, a large commodities trader, to market niobium, scandium, titanium, and rare earth products.

The strategy is to position Elk Creek as a domestic, ESG-compliant supplier at a time when the U.S. is seeking to reduce reliance on China for critical minerals.

### Moat

The moat is asset-specific, not operational. Elk Creek is one of the few large, U.S.-based critical minerals deposits with a NI 43-101 resource and feasibility study. The project has strategic value because it could produce minerals that are on the U.S. critical minerals list and are currently dominated by foreign supply chains. Until production begins, the moat is theoretical and depends on successful execution.

## 3. Valuation

Valuing a pre-revenue mining project is inherently scenario-based. The stock is a call option on the Elk Creek Project reaching production. We use a probability-weighted NAV framework rather than traditional P/E or EV/EBITDA multiples.

| Scenario | Probability | Implied Target | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Bull** | 25% | \$10–\$14 | Full financing closed, additional offtake agreements signed, construction begins, and commodity prices remain supportive. |
| **Base** | 50% | \$6–\$9 | Project financing closes, but with some dilution or delays; first production pushed toward 2028–2029. |
| **Bear** | 25% | \$2–\$4 | Financing fails, commodity prices weaken, or permitting/technical issues delay the project indefinitely. |

### Key Valuation Drivers

- **Project NPV:** The 2022 feasibility study outlined project economics. Updated feasibility with rare earths and improved titanium recovery could materially change the NPV.
- **Commodity Prices:** Niobium, scandium, and titanium prices are opaque and thinly traded; small changes in price assumptions create large swings in project value.
- **Financing Terms:** Debt/equity mix, dilution, and cost of capital will determine how much value accrues to equity holders.
- **Timeline:** Construction and ramp-up delays compress NPV.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **Project Financing Close** — The most important near-term catalyst. NioCorp has stated it is targeting full project financing in 2026, with EXIM and other lenders potentially involved.
2. **Additional Offtake Agreements** — The Traxys agreement is non-binding. Firm, binding offtake contracts with end-users or traders would de-risk revenue and improve debt capacity.
3. **Updated Feasibility Study** — Incorporating rare earths and the improved titanium recovery rates could materially upgrade project economics.
4. **U.S. Policy Support** — Critical minerals are a bipartisan priority. Grants, loans, or tax incentives from the U.S. government could lower capital costs.
5. **Construction Start** — Breaking ground would transform the narrative from a development story to a build-and-ramp story.

## 5. Risk Factors

- **Financing Risk:** The project requires ~\$1.3B in capital. If equity financing is needed, dilution could be significant.
- **Execution Risk:** NioCorp has no operating history as a producer. Mining, processing, and ramp-up to nameplate capacity are complex and often delayed.
- **Commodity Price Risk:** Niobium and scandium are not transparently priced markets. A downturn in steel, aerospace, or EV demand would hurt the project economics.
- **Offtake Risk:** The Traxys agreement is non-binding. If counterparties walk away, project financing could be harder to secure.
- **Regulatory / Permitting Risk:** While land acquisition is complete, additional permits, environmental reviews, or legal challenges could delay construction.
- **Concentration Risk:** The entire investment thesis rests on a single project in Nebraska.

## 6. Recommendation

**Rating: SPEC. BUY**

The risk/reward is asymmetric for investors comfortable with pre-revenue development plays. At \$4.79, the market cap is small relative to the potential value of a producing Elk Creek, but the path to production is uncertain and likely requires significant additional capital. This is a speculative position, not a core holding.

## 7. Sentiment Analysis

Sentiment is cautiously optimistic and driven by recent news flow rather than fundamentals. The Traxys offtake agreement and the company’s focus on U.S. critical minerals supply chains have generated positive attention on financial news outlets, Seeking Alpha, and mining-focused publications. However, the company is still pre-revenue and heavily dependent on financing, so sentiment can swing quickly on capital-raising news.

Social media and retail investor sentiment appears mixed: bullish on the critical minerals and U.S. reshoring narrative, but skeptical of the capital intensity and timeline. Options flow is not a significant factor given the small market cap. Overall sentiment score: **neutral-to-bullish**, with a bias toward bullish if financing closes in 2026.

## 8. Readability Pass

NioCorp is trying to build a mine in Nebraska that produces metals most people have never heard of — niobium, scandium, and titanium. These metals matter for steel, airplanes, and batteries. The U.S. wants more of them produced domestically, and NioCorp has a big deposit. The catch: building the mine will cost more than \$1 billion, the company is not yet making money, and it still needs to line up financing. If it succeeds, the stock could be worth multiples of today’s price. If financing fails or the project stalls, the downside is significant. This is a speculative bet on a domestic critical-minerals project.
