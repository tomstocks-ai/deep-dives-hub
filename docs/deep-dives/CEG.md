## 2. COMPANY OVERVIEW

### Business Model and Revenue Segments

Constellation Energy Corporation (NASDAQ: CEG) is the largest producer of carbon-free energy in the United States and the nation's leading nuclear power operator. The company generates, transmits, and sells electricity to wholesale and retail customers across competitive energy markets, with a particular focus on zero-carbon nuclear generation that serves as reliable baseload power for the grid.

### Industry Position and Competitive Moat

Constellation operates the largest nuclear fleet in the United States — 21 reactors across 12 sites totaling approximately 22 GW of nuclear capacity. In 2025, this fleet produced 183 TWh of zero-emissions electricity with a capacity factor of 94.7%, among the highest in the world. The company also owns and operates natural gas, wind, and solar assets, bringing total pre-Calpine capacity to ~31.7 GW.

**Competitive Advantages:**

- **Scale and Reliability:** Largest U.S. nuclear operator with best-in-class capacity factors (~95%), providing unmatched baseload reliability that intermittent renewables cannot match
- **Zero-Carbon Positioning:** Nuclear generation produces zero carbon emissions during operation, positioning Constellation as a critical enabler of corporate net-zero commitments and AI data center sustainability goals
- **Regulatory Relationships:** Decades of Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) engagement and operational expertise create significant barriers to entry
- **Data Center Power Agreements:** Long-term power supply agreements with major hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon) for AI data center expansion, including a 380 MW deal with CyrusOne

**Competitive Moat Assessment:** STRONG

3. **Nuclear Uprates and License Extensions:** The NRC has approved uprate programs at key facilities, increasing output without new construction. License extensions to 60+ years secure decades of cash flow from existing assets.

4. **Carbon Credits and Inflation Reduction Act (IRA):** Nuclear production tax credits (PTCs) under the IRA provide \$15–18/MWh in additional revenue through 2032, improving fleet economics and supporting reinvestment.

5. **Retail Customer Growth:** Constellation's retail business benefits from customer migration to fixed-rate contracts as power price volatility increases, providing stable, recurring cash flows.

## 6. RISKS

1. **Regulatory and Political Risk:** Nuclear operations are heavily regulated by the NRC. Any safety incidents, license revocation, or adverse regulatory changes could severely impact operations and valuation.

2. **Power Price Volatility:** Wholesale power prices are sensitive to natural gas prices, weather, and grid demand. A sustained decline in power prices would compress margins and reduce the value of generation assets.

3. **Calpine Integration Risk:** The \$16.4B acquisition is the largest in company history. Integration challenges, cost overruns, or cultural mismatches could derail synergy targets and strain the balance sheet.

4. **Nuclear Waste and Decommissioning Liabilities:** Long-term nuclear waste storage and plant decommissioning obligations represent significant off-balance-sheet liabilities that could require unexpected capital outlays.

5. **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** As a capital-intensive utility with significant debt, rising interest rates increase refinancing costs and reduce the present value of long-term cash flows.

6. **Competition from Renewables + Storage:** While nuclear provides baseload advantages, falling costs of solar, wind, and battery storage could erode the economic case for nuclear in certain markets over the long term.

## 8. READABILITY PASS

**Nuclear Capacity Factor:** The percentage of time a nuclear plant actually produces electricity compared to its maximum possible output. Constellation's ~95% capacity factor means its reactors run almost continuously, unlike solar or wind which depend on weather.

**Power Purchase Agreement (PPA):** A long-term contract between a power generator (like Constellation) and a buyer (like a data center) that locks in electricity prices for 10–20 years. PPAs provide revenue visibility and reduce price risk for both parties.

**Baseload Power:** Electricity that is generated continuously, 24/7, to meet the minimum level of demand on the grid. Nuclear is the only zero-carbon source of baseload power at scale.

**Uprate:** Increasing the maximum power output of an existing nuclear reactor through equipment upgrades and NRC approval — essentially getting more electricity from the same plant without building new infrastructure.
