## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

### Business Model and Revenue Segments

Coinbase Global is the largest U.S. cryptocurrency exchange, commanding approximately 55% of the domestic retail market share. Founded in 2012 and publicly listed in April 2021, the platform serves as a critical on-ramp for both retail and institutional investors into digital assets. The company operates a regulated, full-stack infrastructure spanning trading, custody, staking, and stablecoin issuance.

**Revenue Segment Breakdown:**

| Segment | % of Revenue | Description |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Transaction Fees** | ~40% | Trading fees from retail and institutional spot trading; highly cyclical |
| **Subscription & Services** | ~45% | Staking, custody, Earn, and institutional services |
| **Stablecoin Revenue** | ~15% | Interest income from USDC reserves (revenue share with Circle) |

### Industry Position and Competitive Moat

**Competitive Advantages:**

- Regulatory moat: Deepening via GENIUS Act stablecoin legislation and multi-state licensing
- ETF custody dominance: Custodian for 9 of 11 spot Bitcoin ETFs and 8 of 9 Ether ETFs
- Institutional relationships: ~80-82% of total trading volume is institutional
- USDC partnership: Joint venture with Circle; USDC supply at all-time highs (~\$19B average held)

**Competitive Moat Assessment:** MODERATE — Regulatory complexity creates barriers, but fee compression and competition from decentralized and offshore exchanges remain threats.

### Management Team Track Record

**Key Executives:**

- **Brian Armstrong:** Co-founder & CEO (since 2012)
- **Alesia Haas:** CFO

Armstrong has navigated Coinbase through multiple crypto cycles, regulatory scrutiny, and the company's direct listing. The team has demonstrated resilience in adapting business models toward recurring revenue.

## 4. GROWTH CATALYSTS

1. **GENIUS Act Passage:** Bipartisan stablecoin legislation would legitimize USDC, deepen Coinbase's regulatory moat, and unlock institutional stablecoin adoption.

2. **Crypto Bull Market Recovery:** Transaction revenue is highly levered to trading volumes. A sustained BTC/ETH rally would drive outsized revenue and earnings expansion.

3. **ETF Custody Expansion:** Coinbase already custodies 9 of 11 spot Bitcoin ETFs and 8 of 9 Ether ETFs. Additional ETF launches (e.g., Solana, XRP) would drive custody and trading fees.

4. **Institutional Services Growth:** Coinbase Prime, staking, and institutional custody are sticky, high-margin revenue streams with multi-year contracts.

5. **International Expansion:** Derivatives and offshore markets (e.g., Bermuda, Singapore) offer growth outside U.S. regulatory constraints.

---

## 5. RISKS

1. **Regulatory Uncertainty:** SEC enforcement actions, shifting political winds, and state-level licensing create headline risk and potential fines.

2. **Fee Compression:** Retail trading fees face downward pressure from competition (Robinhood, offshore exchanges) and market maker dynamics.

3. **Crypto Winter Volume Collapse:** Transaction revenue is the most volatile segment; a prolonged bear market would severely impact profitability.

4. **Stablecoin Revenue Concentration:** USDC interest income depends on Federal Reserve rates and Circle revenue-sharing terms; rate cuts would compress this stream.

5. **Security & Operational Risk:** Exchange hacks, system outages, or custody failures could damage brand and trigger regulatory action.
