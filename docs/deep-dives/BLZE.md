## 1. Executive Summary

Backblaze, Inc. (NASDAQ: BLZE) is a cloud storage and backup provider offering two primary segments: **B2 Cloud Storage** (object storage for developers, businesses, and AI/ML workloads) and **Computer Backup** (consumer/SMB backup). The company differentiates itself through transparent pricing, no egress fees, and a simple API — positioning as a cost-effective alternative to hyperscalers like AWS S3.

Q1 2026 results demonstrated accelerating momentum: revenue of \$38.7M (+12% YoY) beat guidance, gross margin expanded to 61% (from 56% YoY), and the company posted \$0.04 EPS — a significant beat versus the -\$0.02 consensus. B2 Cloud Storage ARR grew 28% YoY to \$93.0M, while total ARR reached \$158.2M (+13% YoY). The stock has rebounded from 52-week lows near \$3.31 to ~\$8.27, but remains well below its \$10.75 high with an RSI(14) of 61.49 indicating moderate momentum.

We rate BLZE **SPEC. BUY** based on B2's high growth, margin expansion, and AI storage demand tailwinds, balanced against risks from hyperscaler competition, legacy backup drag, and small-scale execution challenges.

## 2. Business Model & Segments

### B2 Cloud Storage
- **Product:** Object storage for developers, businesses, and AI/ML workloads
- **Growth:** 28% YoY ARR growth (\$93.0M ARR as of Q1 2026)
- **Margin Profile:** Higher-margin segment with expanding contribution
- **Competitive Position:** Competes with AWS S3, Wasabi, Cloudflare R2
- **Key Differentiator:** Transparent pricing, no egress fees, simple API
- **AI Tailwind:** AI/ML workload storage demand cited as growth driver in Q1 materials

### Computer Backup
- **Product:** Consumer and SMB backup solution
- **Status:** Declining legacy business, lower-margin
- **Dynamics:** Offsetting drag on overall growth; management focused on stabilizing

### Revenue Model
- Subscription-based SaaS with monthly and annual plans
- B2 charges based on storage used and API operations (no egress fees)
- Computer Backup is a flat-fee unlimited backup model

## 4. Investment Thesis

**Why Backblaze Matters:**
1. **B2 is a high-growth, high-margin engine** — 28% ARR growth with expanding gross margins positions BLZE as a beneficiary of cloud data explosion and AI/ML storage demand.
2. **Margin expansion story is real** — Gross margin improved from 56% to 61% YoY, driven by extended equipment depreciation lives and mix shift toward B2. Path to 65% is plausible.
3. **Profitable inflection underway** — Q1 EPS of \$0.04 vs. -\$0.02 estimate signals the business is approaching breakeven. Positive operating cash flow and narrowing adjusted FCF burn support this trajectory.
4. **AI storage demand tailwind** — AI/ML workloads require vast, cost-effective object storage. Backblaze's no-egress-fee model is attractive for data-heavy AI pipelines.
5. **Attractive valuation for a SaaS growth story** — ~3x ARR with 28% B2 growth and margin expansion is inexpensive relative to cloud infrastructure peers.
