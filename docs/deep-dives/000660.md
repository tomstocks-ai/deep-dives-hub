## 1. Company Overview

SK hynix Inc. (stylized in lowercase) is a South Korean semiconductor memory manufacturer and one of the "Big Three" global memory producers alongside Samsung Electronics and Micron Technology. The company designs, manufactures, and sells dynamic random-access memory (DRAM) chips and NAND flash memory, with a rapidly growing portfolio in high-bandwidth memory (HBM) for artificial intelligence accelerators.

**Business Model & Revenue Segments:**
- **DRAM (~60–70% of revenue):** Standard DDR5/DDR4 for servers, PCs, and mobile devices. The server DRAM segment is the growth engine, driven by AI training and inference clusters.
- **NAND Flash (~30–35% of revenue):** SSDs and storage solutions for enterprise, client, and consumer markets. Includes enterprise NVMe SSDs for data centers.
- **HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** A sub-category of DRAM that has become the crown jewel. HBM3E and upcoming HBM4 are stacked-memory solutions used exclusively by NVIDIA and other AI chipmakers. SK hynix holds an estimated 50–55% global HBM market share and is reportedly supplying roughly two-thirds of NVIDIA's HBM4 orders.

**Competitive Moat:**
SK hynix's moat rests on three pillars: (1) **technology leadership in HBM stacking**, where it beat Samsung to mass production of HBM3E and is the first-to-market leader for HBM4; (2) **vertical integration** from wafer fabrication through packaging, with proprietary through-silicon via (TSV) technology; and (3) **NVIDIA supply-chain lock-in**, including a multiyear memory pact announced in June 2026 covering HBM for Vera Rubin, RTX Spark desktop AI PCs, and Jetson Thor modules. The company's M15X fab and Yongin Cluster expansion are purpose-built for HBM scale.

**Management:**
The company operates under the SK Group conglomerate umbrella. CEO Kwak No-jeong has steered the company through the 2022–2023 memory downturn into the current AI-driven supercycle. Capital allocation is aggressive but justified: FY2026 capex is expected to rise significantly over 2025, with the majority directed to EUV equipment procurement and the Yongin/M15X infrastructure buildout.

## 3. Valuation

Valuing SK hynix requires framing it as a cyclical memory business currently enjoying a structural AI demand uplift. The stock trades at ~2,764,000 KRW per share.

| Scenario | Price Target (KRW) | Assumptions |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Bull** | 3,500,000–3,800,000 | HBM4 demand sustains through 2027, NVIDIA Rubin/Blackwell cycle extends, Samsung HBM4 delays continue, DRAM industry supply discipline holds. P/E ~8–9x on elevated earnings. |
| **Base** | 2,800,000–3,200,000 | HBM growth moderates but remains strong, standard DRAM pricing normalizes gradually, margins compress from 70%+ to 40–50% range. P/E ~10–12x on normalized earnings. |
| **Bear** | 1,800,000–2,200,000 | DRAM oversupply returns in 2027, Samsung catches up in HBM4, AI capex cycle peaks and cuts, memory prices collapse 30–40%. P/E ~15x on trough earnings. |

**Multiples Context:**
At Q1 2026 run-rate earnings, the stock appears cheap on a trailing P/E basis. However, memory is a deeply cyclical industry where peak earnings are not sustainable. The key question is whether AI-driven HBM demand creates a "higher floor" for this cycle compared to prior DRAM booms. Current valuation implies the market believes the floor is higher but not that peak margins are permanent.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **HBM4 Ramp (H2 2026–2027):** SK hynix is the dominant supplier for NVIDIA's HBM4 needs. Mass production is accelerating to February 2026. The 16-Hi HBM4 stack (48GB capacity, 1.5+ TB/s bandwidth) is a generational leap that commands premium pricing.

2. **NVIDIA Multiyear Pact (June 2026):** The announced supply agreement covering Vera Rubin, RTX Spark, and Jetson Thor de-risks revenue visibility for 2026–2028. This is not a one-quarter phenomenon.

3. **Yongin Cluster & M15X Fab:** These greenfield facilities are purpose-built for HBM at scale. First-phase Yongin production begins 2027, adding long-term capacity without the constraints of legacy DRAM fabs.

4. **Server DRAM Upgrade Cycle:** DDR5 adoption and AI server buildouts (not just HBM) drive standard DRAM demand. Data center memory content per server is rising.

5. **Industry Supply Discipline:** The "Big Three" (Samsung, SK hynix, Micron) have demonstrated unprecedented supply restraint since 2023. If this holds, pricing volatility decreases and margin floors rise.

## 5. Risk Factors

1. **DRAM Cyclicality:** Memory is historically one of the most cyclical semiconductor segments. The current 70%+ operating margins are almost certainly unsustainable. When the cycle turns, earnings can collapse 60–80%.

2. **Samsung Competition:** Samsung is aggressively chasing HBM4 share. If Samsung accelerates mass production and closes the technology gap, SK hynix's pricing power erodes. Reports suggest Samsung is targeting early delivery to win back NVIDIA share.

3. **AI Capex Cut Risk:** If hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon) slow AI infrastructure spending — whether due to ROI concerns, model efficiency gains, or macro pressure — HBM demand is the first lever pulled.

4. **China Demand & Export Controls:** China is a major memory consumer. Any expansion of U.S. chip restrictions to HBM or DRAM could impact demand. Conversely, approval to sell H200 to China (as reported in late 2025) was a tailwind.

5. **KRW/USD & Geopolitical:** As a KRW-denominated stock, foreign investors face currency risk. Korean geopolitical tensions (North Korea, China-Taiwan) are background risks.

6. **Capex Intensity:** The Yongin Cluster and M15X require massive capital. If returns disappoint or the cycle turns mid-build, ROIC suffers.

## 7. Readability Pass

**DRAM:** Dynamic Random-Access Memory — the main type of memory chip in computers and servers. "Dynamic" means it needs constant refreshing; "random-access" means any data location can be reached directly.

**NAND Flash:** A type of non-volatile memory (keeps data without power) used in SSDs, phones, and USB drives. Cheaper per gigabyte than DRAM but slower.

**HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** A special type of DRAM where memory chips are stacked vertically and connected to a processor via an ultra-fast interface. Essential for AI GPUs because training large models requires moving enormous amounts of data between memory and compute.

**TSV (Through-Silicon Via):** Vertical electrical connections that pass through silicon wafers, enabling the stacking of memory chips in HBM.

**EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet):** A cutting-edge lithography technology used to print the smallest features on advanced chips. SK hynix is investing heavily in EUV equipment for next-generation memory.

**Capex:** Capital expenditure — money spent on physical assets like factories and equipment.

**Memory Cycle:** The recurring pattern of boom and bust in the memory chip industry. When demand is strong and supply is tight, prices and profits soar. When supply catches up or demand weakens, prices crash and profits evaporate.
