# CRM — Salesforce, Inc.

Salesforce (NYSE: CRM) is the world's largest customer relationship management platform and one of the largest enterprise software companies, now repositioning itself as the "operating system for the Agentic Enterprise." Despite delivering FY2026 revenue of \$41.5B (+10%), record margins, \$14.4B of free cash flow, and an AI/data business (Agentforce + Data 360) whose ARR has tripled year-over-year to \$3.4B, the stock has fallen ~40% from its 52-week high of ~\$277 to \$166.11 (2026-07-02 close) on fears that agentic AI will erode seat-based SaaS pricing. At ~11.8x FY27 non-GAAP EPS guidance with a ~10% trailing FCF yield and a debt-funded \$25B accelerated buyback retiring ~12% of the share count, we think the bear case is substantially priced in while the reported numbers keep improving. **Rating: BUY. Bull \$250–\$280 · Base \$195–\$215 · Bear \$125–\$140.**

## Company Overview

**Business model.** Salesforce sells subscription-based enterprise software across a unified platform ("Customer 360"): Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, Commerce, Slack, Tableau, MuleSoft, and — since late FY2026 — Informatica. Subscription and support is ~95% of revenue (\$39.4B of \$41.5B in FY26) with the remainder professional services. Revenue visibility is exceptional: remaining performance obligation (RPO) stands at \$67.9B, with current RPO (cRPO, next 12 months) of \$33.6B, up 14% YoY as of Q1 FY27.

**The agentic pivot.** The strategic story is Agentforce — autonomous AI agents built on the Salesforce data and workflow layer — plus Data 360 (formerly Data Cloud) as the data foundation. Key disclosures as of Q1 FY27 (April 2026):

- Agentforce ARR of **\$1.2B, up 205% YoY**; combined AI + Data ARR of **~\$3.4B, up over 200% YoY** (includes \$1.1B Informatica Cloud ARR)
- 3.8B Agentic Work Units (AWUs) delivered to date, **+111% Q/Q**; 28.6T tokens processed, +152% Q/Q
- More than 50% of Agentforce and Data 360 bookings from existing customers; premium Agentforce SKU bookings up ~60% YoY
- Slack MCP (Model Context Protocol) passed 1M active users within six weeks of launch
- Public Sector Industry Cloud ARR above \$2B, +23% YoY

**Moat.** Salesforce's moat is the system-of-record position: decades of customer data, deep workflow integration, a large ISV/AppExchange ecosystem, and high switching costs. The agentic strategy leverages exactly this — agents need governed access to customer data and workflows, which is what Salesforce (plus Informatica/Zero Copy) owns. The bet is that AI shifts value **to** the data/workflow layer rather than commoditizing it.

**Management.** Marc Benioff remains Chair and CEO. Robin Washington serves as President and Chief Financial and Operating Officer. The FY26–FY27 posture is "profitable growth": disciplined opex, rising margins every year since FY23, and aggressive capital return — a sharp contrast to the pre-2023 acquisition-led era.

The EPS growth outpacing revenue reflects margin expansion plus the collapsing share count.

**Balance sheet.** Cash and marketable securities were ~\$9.6B at FY26 year-end (down from ~\$14.0B, reflecting the Informatica acquisition and buybacks). In Q1 FY27 Salesforce issued **\$25B of debt to fund an accelerated share repurchase** — a deliberate re-levering of what was historically a near-net-cash balance sheet. This is the single biggest change to the financial profile: interest expense now caps FY27 operating/free cash flow growth guidance at ~4–5%.

**Cash flow.** FY26 operating cash flow was \$15.0B (+15%) and free cash flow \$14.4B (+16%) — a ~35% FCF margin. Q1 FY27 OCF was \$6.7B (+3%) and FCF \$6.6B (+4%) (Q1 is seasonally the largest collections quarter). Capex remains light at ~1.5% of revenue guided for FY27 — Salesforce rents rather than builds AI infrastructure.

**Capital returns.** FY26: \$14.3B returned (\$12.7B buybacks, \$1.6B dividends), then a new **\$50B repurchase authorization**. Q1 FY27 alone: **\$27.5B returned**, dominated by the \$25B ASR with 103M shares (~11% of the company) delivered upfront and final settlement in Q3 FY27. The quarterly dividend is \$0.44 (+5.8% YoY, ~1.1% yield). Diluted shares fell from 956M (FY26 average) to ~819M outstanding today — buybacks are now overwhelming SBC dilution rather than merely offsetting it.

**Scenario framework** (anchored on FY27 non-GAAP EPS guidance of \$14.06–\$14.12; targets match `CRM.json`):

- **Bull \$250–\$280 (~18–20x forward EPS).** H2 FY27 organic acceleration prints as guided; Agentforce ARR keeps compounding toward \$2B+ and consumption revenue (AWUs) becomes a disclosed growth driver; the "AI kills SaaS" narrative breaks and the multiple mean-reverts toward (still below) its historical range. The 52-week high (~\$277) marks the top of the range; the \$246 street average sits inside it.
- **Base \$195–\$215 (~14–15x).** FY27 lands on guidance (\$45.9–46.2B, +11%; non-GAAP OM 34.3%), organic growth holds ~8–9%, Agentforce scales but stays a single-digit share of revenue. Partial re-rate plus the ~12% share-count reduction do the work.
- **Bear \$125–\$140 (~9–10x).** Seat erosion becomes visible in renewals, organic growth slides to mid-single digits, cRPO decelerates below 10%, and the new \$25B debt load turns from accretive to constraining. Note the stock already traded to \$146–150 in this narrative's worst moments — the bear case is not hypothetical, it is roughly the April 2026 low.

## Growth Catalysts

1. **Agentforce consumption ramp.** \$1.2B ARR growing 205% YoY with pricing anchored in Agentic Work Units — a shift from per-seat to consumption economics that, if it scales, directly refutes the seat-erosion bear case. 29,000+ deals closed since launch; premium SKU bookings +60% YoY.
2. **Data 360 + Informatica.** Data ingestion doubled YoY (112T records in FY26, 53T via Zero Copy). Informatica adds \$1.1B of cloud ARR and enterprise data-governance credibility — the plumbing agents require. ~3pts of FY27 revenue growth comes from Informatica, with cross-sell upside beyond.
3. **Guided H2 FY27 organic revenue acceleration** in Sales, Service, Slack, Agentforce, and Data 360 — management put this marker down publicly in May 2026; hitting it is the clearest near-term re-rating catalyst.
4. **Capital-return flywheel.** \$25B ASR (final settlement Q3 FY27) plus the remainder of the \$50B authorization: at ~\$166 every incremental \$10B retires ~7% of the float, compounding EPS regardless of multiple.
5. **Margin runway.** Non-GAAP OM guided to 34.3% in FY27, up ~1,100 bps from FY22; management reiterates FY30 targets with continued expansion as AI drives internal efficiency.
6. **Catalyst calendar:** Q2 FY27 earnings (September 2026) → ASR final settlement (Q3 FY27) → Dreamforce (October 2026) → Q3 FY27 earnings, the H2-acceleration checkpoint (December 2026) → FY27 full-year results and FY28 guide (February–March 2027).

## Risk Factors

**Business / thesis risks**

- **Agentic disruption of seat pricing (the core bear case).** If AI agents let customers do the same work with fewer human users, per-seat revenue in Sales and Service Cloud erodes at renewal. Salesforce's answer — sell the agents, price by consumption — is scaling fast but from a base that is still under 8% of revenue.
- **Competition.** Microsoft (Copilot + Dynamics), ServiceNow, HubSpot down-market, and AI-native startups are all attacking agent workloads; foundation-model vendors keep moving up the stack.
- **Informatica integration** (\$8B, closed FY26): execution and margin dilution risk, plus the optics of returning to M&A.

**Financial risks**

- **Re-levered balance sheet.** \$25B of ASR debt against ~\$9–11B of cash flips Salesforce to a meaningful net-debt position; FY27 FCF growth guidance was cut to ~4–5% for the interest impact. Further large buybacks depend on debt markets staying friendly.
- **SBC and earnings quality.** Stock-based compensation runs ~\$3.6B/yr (~\$3.64/share of the GAAP-to-non-GAAP bridge); the buyback now more than offsets dilution, but non-GAAP EPS remains the market's yardstick.

**Macro / sector risks**

- Enterprise software budgets remain under scrutiny; seat-count rationalization in a downturn would compound the structural fear.
- Sector-wide multiple compression: the software cohort has de-rated as capital rotated to AI infrastructure; CRM cannot fully re-rate if the whole group stays out of favor.

## Readability Pass

Plain English: Salesforce is the biggest maker of software that companies use to manage sales, customer service, and marketing. Its stock has dropped about 40% in a year — not because business is bad (it grew 10%, earns record profits, and generated \$14 billion of spare cash), but because investors worry that AI agents will let companies buy fewer software "seats," shrinking Salesforce's core business over time. Salesforce's counter-move is to sell the AI agents itself (a product called Agentforce, already at \$1.2 billion a year in recurring revenue and tripling), and to borrow \$25 billion to buy back its own shares at what management clearly considers fire-sale prices — retiring roughly one share in eight. You are paying about 12 times next year's guided earnings for a business still growing 11% with best-in-class cash generation. If the AI fear proves overdone, the stock has 25–60% upside to our base and bull ranges; if the fear is right and growth stalls, you lose maybe 20% from here. We think those odds favor buying, hence the BUY rating — while respecting that this only works if the next two or three quarters show the promised re-acceleration.
