# PAAS — Pan American Silver Corp.

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Pan American Silver Corp. is the world's largest primary silver producer by volume and a significant gold producer, with operations spanning Mexico, Peru, Bolivia, Argentina, Canada, and Guatemala. The company produced 22.8 million attributable silver ounces in FY2025, exceeding raised guidance of 22.0–22.5 million ounces, and also generated substantial gold output from assets like La Arena, Shahuindo, and Timmins.

**Business Model:** Revenue is driven by silver (~55% of metal sales) and gold (~40%), with lead and zinc by-product credits. The company operates a diversified portfolio of underground and open-pit mines, plus the Juanicipio joint venture (56% owned, with Fresnillo) which began contributing materially in 2025 and significantly lowered silver-segment AISC to \$14.50–\$16/oz.

**Competitive Moat:**

- **Scale:** Largest primary silver producer globally — unmatched leverage to silver price moves.
- **Diversification:** 10+ operating mines across multiple jurisdictions reduces single-asset risk.
- **Juanicipio:** World-class high-grade silver deposit (500+ g/t Ag) with low cash costs transforms the cost curve.
- **Reserve base:** Proven and probable reserves of ~550M oz silver and ~5M oz gold provide multi-decade visibility.

**Management:** CEO Michael Steinmann has focused on operational consolidation, cost discipline, and unlocking value from the Juanicipio JV. The company returned capital via dividends and has maintained a conservative balance sheet despite acquisition history.

### Income Statement

|--------|--------|--------|------------|
| Revenue | ~\$4.1B | ~\$3.5B | +17% |
| Net Income | ~\$420M | ~\$180M | +133% |

| Silver Production | 22.8M oz | 21.2M oz | +7.5% |
| Gold Production | ~850k oz | ~820k oz | +3.7% |

Q4 2025 was a record quarter: 7.3M oz silver production, revenue of \$1.18B (+7.3% above consensus), and adjusted EPS of \$1.11 (+30.6% above forecast). The Juanicipio ramp-up drove a step-change in silver volumes and margins.

### Balance Sheet
- **Cash:** ~\$450M (year-end 2025)
- **Debt:** ~\$1.2B total; manageable given EBITDA generation
- **Net debt/EBITDA:** ~0.7x — improving rapidly with higher metal prices

### Cash Flow
- **Operating Cash Flow:** ~\$800M in FY2025
- **Free Cash Flow:** ~\$300M after sustaining capital and exploration
- **Dividend:** ~\$0.40/share annually (~0.9% yield at current price)

### Segment Margins
Silver-segment AISC fell to \$14.50–\$16/oz in 2025 (from \$17–\$19/oz previously) thanks to Juanicipio's low-cost output. At \$36/oz spot silver, this implies a segment margin of \$20–\$21/oz — exceptionally wide by historical standards.

## 4. CATALYSTS

- **Silver price momentum** — Supply deficit, solar demand, and monetary hedge flows could push spot silver above \$40/oz.
- **Juanicipio ramp-up** — Reaching design capacity of 12M oz/year (100% basis) drives further cost reductions and volume growth.
- **Escobal restart** — Guatemala's Escobal mine (suspended since 2017) is one of the world's largest silver deposits. A favorable court ruling or permit resolution would be transformative.
- **M&A optionality** — PAAS has a history of accretive acquisitions (Tahoe Resources, Minefinders). Consolidation in the silver space is likely as majors seek leverage.
- **Critical minerals designation** — U.S. and EU classification of silver as a strategic/critical mineral could unlock subsidies, offtake agreements, and investor flows.

---

## 5. RISKS

- **Commodity price volatility** — Silver is more volatile than gold; a 20% drop in spot prices would crush margins and sentiment.
- **Geopolitical risk** — Operations in Bolivia, Argentina, and Guatemala face regulatory, tax, and social-license risks.
- **Escobal uncertainty** — The Guatemala mine has been suspended for 8 years. There is no clear timeline for resolution, and a negative outcome would remove a key growth option.
- **Cost inflation** — Labor, energy, and consumable costs in mining have risen sharply; sustaining capital requirements are growing.
- **Environmental incidents** — Tailings dam failures or water disputes can halt operations and trigger regulatory action.
- **FX risk** — Stronger local currencies (Mexican peso, Peruvian sol) vs. USD raise local costs.

## 9. READABILITY PASS

- **AISC (All-In Sustaining Costs):** The full cost of producing one ounce of metal, including mining, processing, royalties, and capital needed to maintain current production levels.
- **Attributable production:** The share of metal output that belongs to PAAS after accounting for joint-venture partners (e.g., 56% of Juanicipio).
- **By-product credits:** Revenue from secondary metals (gold, lead, zinc) that reduces the net cost of producing the primary metal (silver).
- **Escobal:** A large silver mine in Guatemala, suspended since 2017 due to community and regulatory disputes. Restart would be a major catalyst.
- **Juanicipio:** A high-grade silver-zinc joint venture with Fresnillo in Mexico. One of the world's highest-grade silver mines.
- **Spot price:** The current market price for immediate delivery of a commodity (e.g., silver at \$36/oz today).
- **Critical mineral:** A material deemed essential for economic or national security. Silver's industrial role makes it a candidate for such designation.
