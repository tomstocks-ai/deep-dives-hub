## 1. Executive Summary

NCR Voyix Corporation (NYSE: VYX) is a fintech and digital payments company focused on providing banking, payments, and commerce solutions to financial institutions, retailers, and restaurants. The company was spun out from NCR Corporation in late 2023, creating a standalone entity focused on software-led digital banking, payments processing, and self-service commerce technologies. At \$5.50, VYX trades near the middle of its 52-week range, with a market cap of approximately \$700M, reflecting investor uncertainty around the standalone execution story and a leveraged balance sheet inherited from the parent.

We assign a **SPEC. BUY** rating based on VYX's entrenched relationships with thousands of banks and credit unions, a large installed base of ATMs and self-service kiosks, and the potential for margin expansion as the company transitions from hardware-centric revenue to higher-margin software and services. However, the stock carries high uncertainty due to significant debt, ongoing restructuring, and a complex integration story.

**Price Targets:**
- **Bull:** \$8–\$9
- **Base:** \$6–\$7
- **Bear:** \$4–\$5

## 2. Business Overview

NCR Voyix was created as part of a strategic separation of NCR Corporation, which split into two publicly traded companies: NCR Voyix (VYX), focused on digital commerce, payments, and banking software, and NCR Atleos (formerly NCR), focused on ATMs and self-service banking hardware. VYX is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, and serves a global customer base.

### Revenue Segments

| Segment | Description | Key Drivers |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Digital Banking** | Online and mobile banking platforms for financial institutions | Deposit growth, digital adoption, SaaS migration |
| **Payments** | Payment processing, merchant acquiring, and transaction services | Transaction volumes, merchant signings, interchange rates |
| **Commerce** | Self-service checkout, POS software, and retail technology | Retail capex cycles, labor cost pressures, SaaS attach |
| **Services & Support** | Maintenance, professional services, and managed services | Installed base size, renewal rates, upsell |

VYX's strategic priority is shifting from transactional hardware revenue to recurring software and payments revenue. The company manages a large installed base of ATMs, self-checkout terminals, and POS systems, providing a captive audience for software and services upsell.

### Geographic Footprint
- **Primary:** United States (~60% of revenue)
- **Secondary:** Europe, Latin America, Asia-Pacific
- International expansion is a medium-term priority, with particular focus on digital banking SaaS in emerging markets.

- **Cash Flow Inflection:** Management has guided to positive and growing free cash flow as restructuring completes and capex normalizes.

## 4. Investment Thesis

### Why VYX?

1. **Installed Base Moat:** VYX serves thousands of banks, credit unions, and retailers with deeply embedded technology. Switching costs for core banking and payments infrastructure are high, creating sticky recurring revenue.

2. **Software Transition:** The shift from hardware sales to SaaS and recurring payments revenue is structurally positive for margins and valuation multiples. Every 1% of revenue mix shift toward software adds meaningful EBITDA.

3. **Payments Growth:** The payments segment benefits from secular tailwinds in digital transactions, contactless payments, and merchant acquiring. VYX is investing in modern payment rails to capture this growth.

4. **Deleveraging Optionality:** As debt is paid down from free cash flow, equity value should re-rate. A successful refinancing could also reduce interest expense and improve net income.

5. **Takeover Target:** At ~\$700M market cap with strategic assets in digital banking and payments, VYX could attract acquisition interest from larger fintech or private equity players.

| **Market Cap** | ~\$700M | ~\$70B | ~\$15B | ~\$12B |

### Valuation Commentary
- VYX's ~0.25x P/S is extremely depressed and reflects skepticism about the standalone story. If the company successfully transitions to a software/services model, multiple expansion to 0.5–1.0x P/S is reasonable.
- **Base Case (\$6–\$7):** Assumes modest revenue stabilization, 100bps margin expansion, and debt paydown. Implies EV/EBITDA of ~9–10x.
- **Bull Case (\$8–\$9):** Requires faster software growth, successful cost restructuring, and a debt refinancing that reduces interest expense. Implies EV/EBITDA of ~11–12x and P/S of ~0.5x.
- **Bear Case (\$4–\$5):** Reflects hardware revenue collapse, debt covenant stress, or failed restructuring. The stock would trade near tangible book value with limited downside if asset values are realized.
