## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

### Business Model and Revenue Segments

Everpure, Inc. (formerly Pure Storage, Inc., ticker PSTG) is a leading provider of **all-flash data storage solutions** for enterprises, cloud providers, and government organizations. The company rebranded from Pure Storage to Everpure in February 2026 and changed its NYSE ticker symbol to the single letter **"P"** on April 17, 2026 — a move reflecting its ambition to become the definitive platform for modern data infrastructure.

Everpure's core value proposition is replacing legacy mechanical disk storage with high-performance, energy-efficient flash arrays, coupled with a **subscription-style Evergreen//One** consumption model that decouples hardware refresh cycles from customer budget constraints.

**Revenue Segment Breakdown:**

| Segment | % of Revenue | Description |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Product (FlashArray & FlashBlade)** | ~72% | Hardware sales of all-flash arrays for block, file, and object storage workloads |
| **Subscription Services (Evergreen//One)** | ~25% | Subscription and as-a-service offerings including hardware refresh, software, and support |
| **Professional Services** | ~3% | Implementation, migration, and consulting services |

### Industry Position and Competitive Moat

**Competitive Advantages:**

- **Technology leadership:** Proprietary DirectFlash modules and Purity OS software stack deliver consistent sub-millisecond latency at scale
- **Evergreen//One model:** Subscription approach eliminates forklift upgrades, driving 90%+ gross retention rates
- **Energy efficiency:** All-flash solutions consume up to 10x less power and rack space than legacy disk arrays — a growing enterprise priority
- **Platform consolidation:** Unified platform for block, file, and object workloads reduces data silos versus point-product competitors
- **AI/ML data pipeline positioning:** FlashBlade optimized for unstructured data and AI training workloads

**Competitive Moat Assessment:** MODERATE — Strong product differentiation and sticky subscription model, but faces intense competition from Dell EMC, NetApp, HPE, and cloud-native storage (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud).

### Management Team Track Record

**Key Executives:**

- **Charles Giancarlo:** CEO (since 2017). Formerly CTO at Cisco and managing director at Silver Lake. Led Pure Storage through IPO, profitability inflection, and the Everpure rebrand.
- **Kevan Krysler:** CFO (since 2020). Previously at Autodesk; instrumental in driving operating leverage and FCF inflection.
- **Ajay Singh:** Chief Product Officer. Drives product roadmap for FlashArray, FlashBlade, and Portworx container storage.
- **Shawn Hansen:** Chief Revenue Officer. Leads global sales and the shift to subscription/aaS models.

**Management Assessment:** Giancarlo is a seasoned operator with deep enterprise technology experience. Under his leadership, Everpure has transitioned from growth-at-all-costs to profitable growth — a shift validated by FY2026 GAAP net income of \$188.2M.

## 7. READABILITY PASS

**All-Flash Storage:** Data storage systems that use flash memory (the same technology in USB drives and smartphones) instead of spinning mechanical hard disks. Flash is faster, more reliable, and uses less power — but historically cost more per gigabyte. Everpure's innovation is making all-flash cost-competitive with disk for enterprise workloads.

**Evergreen//One:** Everpure's subscription service where customers pay a recurring fee for storage capacity, hardware upgrades, software, and support. Instead of buying hardware every 3-5 years (a "forklift upgrade"), Everpure continuously refreshes the equipment. This is like leasing a car with free upgrades instead of buying one.

**DirectFlash Module:** Everpure's proprietary flash memory design that optimizes performance and endurance compared to off-the-shelf SSDs. Think of it as a custom engine versus a mass-produced one — designed specifically for Everpure's architecture.

**Convertible Notes:** A type of debt that can be converted into company stock at a predetermined price. It's like a loan that the lender can choose to turn into ownership instead of getting paid back in cash. The \$2.78B convertible notes create potential future dilution for existing shareholders.

**Stock-Based Compensation (SBC):** When companies pay employees with stock or stock options instead of (or in addition to) cash. While it conserves cash, it increases the total number of shares, diluting existing shareholders. Everpure's \$481.7M annual SBC is a significant headwind.

**Gross Margin:** For every \$1 of revenue, how much is left after direct costs (components, manufacturing, shipping). Everpure's 70.4% gross margin means they keep \$0.704 of every dollar — excellent for a hardware business.

**Free Cash Flow (FCF):** The actual cash a business generates after all expenses and investments. Everpure generated \$690.6M in FY2026 FCF — real money that can be used for acquisitions, buybacks, or balance sheet strengthening.

**Beta (1.44):** A measure of stock volatility relative to the overall market. A beta of 1.44 means if the S&P 500 moves 1%, Everpure tends to move 1.44% in the same direction. Higher beta = more volatility.

**Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio:** How much investors pay for each dollar of revenue. Everpure's 7.6x P/S means investors pay \$7.60 for every \$1 of annual sales. For comparison, mature tech hardware trades at 2-4x, while high-growth SaaS trades at 10-20x.

**Unstructured Data:** Data that doesn't fit neatly into rows and columns (like spreadsheets). Think images, videos, sensor data, emails, and documents. FlashBlade is designed for this type of data, which now represents ~80% of all enterprise data.
