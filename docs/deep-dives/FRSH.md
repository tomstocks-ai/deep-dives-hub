# FRSH — Freshworks Inc.

Freshworks is a mid-cap enterprise SaaS vendor whose dual product lines — Employee Experience (EX) via Freshservice and Customer Experience (CX) via Freshdesk — are trading at a discount because legacy CX growth has slowed while EX continues to scale. Q1 2026 results showed 16% revenue growth, 80.8% non-GAAP gross margin, and \$55.4M in free cash flow (24% margin). With EX ARR now above \$540M (+27% YoY), AI attach rates above 65% in new deals, and a freshly streamlined cost structure, the company is positioned to convert its \$935M+ ARR into durable cash-flow compounding. The stock is near the low end of its 52-week range, creating a 25%+ base-case upside to the low-\$11 range.

## 1. Company Overview

Freshworks Inc. (NASDAQ: FRSH) provides cloud-based software for IT service management (ITSM), customer support, and customer engagement. The company is organized around two revenue segments:

- **Employee Experience (EX) — Freshservice.** ITSM, IT asset management (ITAM), and IT operations management (ITOM) tools for internal IT teams. EX is the growth engine, with ARR exceeding \$540M in Q1 2026 (+27% YoY, +25% constant currency).
- **Customer Experience (CX) — Freshdesk and Freshdesk Omni.** Customer support and omni-channel engagement software for external support teams. CX ARR was approximately \$395M (+6% YoY, +4% CC), reflecting maturation and macro pressure on SMB/enterprise support spend.

The company serves more than 67,000 customers globally, with large cohorts growing rapidly: 1,646 customers generate >\$100k ARR (+29% YoY) and 3,938 generate >\$50k ARR (+22% YoY). These larger cohorts account for roughly 55% and 39% of total ARR, respectively, showing a healthy migration upmarket. Freshworks differentiates through product-led growth, intuitive UX, rapid deployment, and transparent pricing, historically competing below the price point of ServiceNow, Salesforce, and Zendesk.

Recent M&A has expanded the addressable footprint. Device42, an IT asset management provider, was integrated into Freshservice in January 2026. FireHydrant, an incident-response and IT operations management vendor, was acquired effective January 1, 2026, and is expected to be integrated through 2026, broadening the ITOM TAM and deepening Freshservice's IT resilience story.

## 2. Financial Analysis

Freshworks reported Q1 2026 (quarter ended March 31, 2026) revenue of \$228.6M, up 16% year-over-year and 15.5% in constant currency. Non-GAAP gross margin was 80.8%, in line with best-in-class SaaS benchmarks. Non-GAAP operating income was \$41M, representing an 18% operating margin, and free cash flow was \$55.4M (24% FCF margin).

| Revenue | \$228.6M | +16% YoY |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 80.8% | Stable |

The year-over-year EPS decline reflects higher investment in AI R&D, sales capacity, and integration costs from recent acquisitions. Management has addressed the cost trajectory by announcing a roughly 11% global headcount reduction in Q2 2026, expected to incur \$8M in one-time restructuring charges while consolidating go-to-market and streamlining R&D. The balance sheet is strong: ~\$780M in cash and investments and no meaningful debt give Freshworks ample capacity to execute the restructuring, integrate FireHydrant, and continue returning capital over time.

Guidance for Q2 2026 calls for revenue of \$232–\$235M (+13–15% YoY), non-GAAP operating income of \$41–\$43M, and non-GAAP EPS of \$0.13 on approximately 280M diluted shares. For the full year 2026, management expects revenue of \$958–\$964M (+14–15% YoY), non-GAAP operating income of \$207–\$215M, and adjusted FCF margin of 24–27% (implying roughly \$250M in annual FCF). Over the next three years, management targets compounding adjusted FCF per share at least 20% annually.

## 3. Valuation

At \$10.74 with ~252.4M shares outstanding, Freshworks carries a market cap of ~\$2.7B and an enterprise value of ~\$2.2B. Against FY2026 revenue guidance of ~\$960M, the stock trades at roughly 2.8x P/S and 2.3x EV/Sales. On ~\$250M of projected adjusted FCF, the FCF yield is ~9%. These multiples are well below the historical SaaS growth-compounder range, reflecting investor concerns about CX growth deceleration, AI-driven pricing disruption, and the recent restructuring.

The valuation argument rests on whether the company can sustain mid-teens revenue growth while expanding margins. If EX continues to grow 20–25% and CX stabilizes in the mid-single digits, the implied FCF compounding supports a higher multiple. Freshworks is not priced for successful execution; it is priced for ongoing slowdown.

- **Bull case — \$14.00–\$16.00:** CX ARR reaccelerates to high-single-digit growth; EX continues above 20%; AI attach and FireHydrant integration drive net revenue retention above 110%; FCF margins approach 30% and the stock re-rates to 4x EV/Sales.
- **Base case — \$11.00–\$12.50:** Revenue grows 14–15%, margins expand modestly, and ~\$250M FCF supports a 2.5–3.0x EV/Sales multiple. This implies ~25–40% upside from current levels.
- **Bear case — \$7.50–\$9.00:** CX ARR stalls or declines; competitive pressure from ServiceNow and Zendesk accelerates churn; restructuring/integration execution falters; multiple compresses to 2x EV/Sales or below.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **Employee Experience momentum.** Freshservice ARR is now the largest product line, growing 27% YoY and carrying higher gross margins. It is the primary reason to own the stock.
2. **AI monetization.** Freddy AI Copilot customer adoption grew over 80% YoY with an attach rate above 65% in new deals >\$30k ARR. The recently announced MCP Gateway lets customers build custom AI agents, opening new monetization opportunities beyond seat-based pricing.
3. **FireHydrant integration.** Completion of the integration in 2026 expands the ITOM TAM and should increase cross-sell into Freshservice accounts.
4. **Margin expansion from restructuring.** The 11% headcount reduction, combined with consolidated go-to-market, should drive operating leverage and set up the company for the 20% annual adjusted FCF-per-share compounding target.
5. **Long-term ARR target.** Management is targeting \$1B+ ARR and has outlined a path to \$1.3B+ revenue and \$425M+ FCF by 2028. Any formal update to this outlook in late 2026 or early 2027 could rerate the stock.
6. **Upcoming earnings catalysts.** Q2 2026 results in early August 2026 and Q3 2026 results in November 2026 are the near-term events that can confirm or challenge the margin-expansion thesis.

## 5. Risk Factors

- **Slowing CX growth.** CX ARR growth of ~6% is a drag on overall revenue. If the company cannot stabilize this segment, headline growth could fall below the mid-teens range.
- **AI disruption of seat-based pricing.** Agentic AI could reduce the number of human seats required, pressuring the traditional per-user SaaS model and pricing power.
- **Competition.** ServiceNow dominates ITSM; Zendesk, HubSpot, Salesforce, and Zoho compete in CX. Larger peers have bigger distribution, more AI investment, and stronger bundling leverage.
- **Execution risk on restructuring.** Workforce reductions can disrupt sales cycles and product roadmaps if not managed carefully.
- **Integration risk.** FireHydrant and Device42 must be integrated without churning their respective customer bases or diluting the Freshworks platform experience.
- **Macro enterprise spend pressure.** Enterprise SaaS buyers remain cautious on discretionary IT spend, lengthening sales cycles and pressuring renewal rates.

## 6. Recommendation

**Rating: BUY.**

Initiate a position at current levels (\$10.50–\$11.25) with a base-case target of \$11.00–\$12.50 and a bull case of \$14.00–\$16.00. The risk/reward is favorable because the stock is already discounting much of the CX slowdown and is pricing in little credit for EX growth, AI attach, and the FCF compounding plan. Use a bear-case floor of \$7.50–\$9.00 as a stop-loss zone; a sustained close below \$7.50 would indicate the restructuring is failing or CX is deteriorating faster than expected.

**Catalyst calendar:**

- **Q2 2026 earnings** (early August 2026) — watch restructuring impact and margin guidance.
- **Q3 2026 earnings** (November 2026) — look for CX stabilization and EX retention.
- **FireHydrant integration completion** (through 2026) — should expand ITOM cross-sell.
- **Long-term outlook update** (late 2026 / early 2027) — potential formalization of the 2028 \$1B+ ARR / \$1.3B+ revenue plan.

Retail sentiment is mixed: the stock is off its 52-week high of \$15.06 and has recovered from a low of \$6.79, creating a wide range of expectations. Short interest is not extreme, but the recent restructuring announcement has drawn attention from both value and growth-oriented investors.
