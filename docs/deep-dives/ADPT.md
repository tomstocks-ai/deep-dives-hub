# ADPT — Adaptive Biotechnologies Corporation

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Adaptive Biotechnologies (NASDAQ: ADPT) is a commercial-stage biotech company that translates the genetics of the adaptive immune system into clinical products to diagnose and treat disease. Founded in 2009 and headquartered in Seattle, the company operates two business units:

- **Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) — ~95% of revenue:** The clonoSEQ assay monitors blood-cancer patients for trace disease after treatment. It is FDA-cleared and CMS-covered for multiple myeloma, B-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL), and chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL). In 2022 the assay expanded into diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) via circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA). Test volume is the primary growth driver.
- **Immune Medicine (IM) — ~5% of revenue:** Uses immune-receptor data to generate insights across immunology. Products include immunoSEQ (research sequencing) and T-Detect (T-cell based disease detection, formerly COVID-focused, now pivoting to other applications). This segment is pre-profitability and consumes capital.

**Competitive moat:** Adaptive has built the largest immune-receptor database in the world (billions of sequences), creating high switching costs for clinicians once a patient is enrolled in clonoSEQ monitoring. The assay is the only FDA-cleared MRD test with broad payer coverage across major lymphoid malignancies.

**Management:** CEO Chad Robins co-founded the company. Insider ownership is meaningful, though recent filings show significant insider selling (~US\\$16M over the past 3 months) — a yellow flag.

## 2. FINANCIAL ANALYSIS

### Income Statement
- **FY2025 revenue:** ~US\\$224M (MRD contributed US\\$212.3M, growing 46% YoY). Total test volume for clonoSEQ reached 105,587.
- **Q1 2026 revenue:** US\$~59M (+35% YoY), with MRD up 53% and test volume up 41% to 32,595 tests. MRD now accounts for 95% of total revenue.
- **Gross margin:** ~65% on MRD — healthy for a diagnostics business.
- **Net income:** Still negative. The company is not forecast to become profitable over the next 3 years. FY2025 net loss was significant, driven by Immune Medicine R&D and commercial infrastructure build-out.

### Balance Sheet
- **Cash:** ~US\\$281M in short-term assets (per WallStreetZen data). This provides roughly 4–5 quarters of runway at the current cash-burn rate.
- **Debt:** Has increased relative to prior periods. Debt covenants exist and could create pressure if revenue growth stalls.
- **Dilution risk:** The company has raised capital in the past and may need to again if the path to profitability extends beyond 2026.

### Cash Flow
- **Operating cash flow:** Negative, but improving as MRD scales.
- **Free cash flow:** Not yet positive. The company has guided toward company-wide profitability metrics in 2026, which likely means adjusted EBITDA or operating cash flow breakeven rather than GAAP net income.
- **Capital intensity:** Low for MRD (lab-based, asset-light). Immune Medicine is the cash sink.

## 3. VALUATION

### Multiples & Metrics
- **Current price:** US\\$18.78 (as of 2026-06-11)
- **Market cap:** ~US\\$2.8B (mid-cap)
- **P/S ratio:** ~12.5x trailing revenue — high, but not unusual for a high-growth diagnostics company approaching profitability.
- **Analyst consensus price target:** US\\$20 (per Simply Wall St, up from US\\$19 recently). Morningstar fair value estimate is US\\$95.71, though this appears to use a long-term DCF that may not align with near-term fundamentals.

## 4. GROWTH CATALYSTS

1. **MRD TAM expansion:** clonoSEQ is expanding into new indications (DLBCL ctDNA launched 2022; additional lymphomas in pipeline). Each new indication expands addressable test volume.
2. **Payor coverage wins:** Broadening CMS and private-payor coverage reduces patient out-of-pocket costs and drives volume.
3. **Immune Medicine partnerships:** A major pharma or biotech partnership for immune-receptor data could validate the platform and provide non-dilutive capital.
4. **Profitability milestone:** The company has explicitly targeted company-wide profitability in 2026. Achieving this would re-rate the stock significantly.
5. **M&A target:** With a unique immune-database asset and a growing MRD franchise, ADPT could be an attractive acquisition target for a larger diagnostics or pharma company.

---

## 5. RISK FACTORS

### Business Risks
- **Customer/payor concentration:** MRD revenue depends on oncology clinics and payor reimbursement policies. A CMS coverage decision against clonoSEQ would be catastrophic.
- **Competition:** Other MRD players (Invitae, Guardant Health, Natera) are developing competing assays. Natera’s Signatera is a direct competitor in the ctDNA MRD space.
- **Immune Medicine failure:** If the IM segment cannot find a viable commercial path, it becomes a perpetual cash drain.

### Financial Risks
- **Liquidity:** ~US\\$281M cash is not infinite. At the current burn rate, the company has 4–5 quarters of runway. A capital raise is likely if profitability is delayed.
- **Dilution:** Past raises have been dilutive. Future raises could be at unfavorable prices if the stock weakens.
- **Debt covenants:** Increasing debt levels create covenant compliance risk if revenue misses.

### Macro/Sector Risks
- **Regulatory:** FDA or CMS policy shifts on MRD testing reimbursement.
- **Biotech sentiment:** The sector is rate-sensitive. A hawkish Fed or risk-off environment hurts high-growth, pre-profitability biotechs.
- **Insider selling:** US\\$16M in insider sales over 3 months is a concern. It may be routine diversification, but it warrants monitoring.

## 9. RECOMMENDATION

**Rating:** HOLD / SPECULATIVE

ADPT is a compelling long-term story with a dominant MRD franchise and a massive immune-receptor database. The 46% MRD growth and clear path to profitability are genuine positives. However, the stock has run hard (+80% over the past year, RSI 71), the analyst consensus target is only US\\$20, and insider selling is notable. The risk/reward at US\\$18.78 is balanced, not asymmetric.

- **Position sizing:** Small (2–3% max). This is a biotech with binary risks (regulatory, competitive, funding).
- **Entry strategy:** Wait for a pullback to US\\$16.00–16.50 (RSI reset to 50–55) for a better entry. If you already own it, hold but do not add here.
- **Stop loss:** US\\$13.50 (–28% from current) — a break below the March 2026 support cluster would signal a deeper correction.
- **Key levels:** Entry US\\$16.50, Target US\\$22, Stop US\\$13.50

## 10. READABILITY & CLARITY PASS

- **MRD (Minimal Residual Disease):** A test that looks for tiny amounts of cancer remaining after treatment. Think of it like a security camera that can spot a single intruder in a stadium — it catches relapse before symptoms appear.
- **Cash burn:** How fast the company is spending its cash pile. At the current rate, ADPT has about 4–5 quarters of money left before needing to raise more.
- **P/S ratio (Price-to-Sales):** How much investors pay for each dollar of revenue. At 12.5x, ADPT is expensive compared to mature companies, but typical for a fast-growing biotech.
- **GAAP net income:** The official accounting profit/loss. ADPT is still losing money by this measure, even though operations are improving.
- **Dilutive raise:** When a company sells new shares to raise cash, existing shareholders own a smaller slice of the pie.
