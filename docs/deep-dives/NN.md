## 1. Company Overview: The GPS Backup Mission

### What They Do
NextNav builds terrestrial PNT systems that work where GPS fails — indoors, in urban canyons, and during jamming/spoofing attacks. Think of it as a ground-based backup to satellite navigation, delivered over their licensed 900 MHz spectrum using standard 5G infrastructure.

### Two Product Lines
| System | Function | Status |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **Pinnacle** | Floor-level altitude accuracy for 911 services | Operational in 4,400 US cities, AT&T partnership extended through Oct 2028 |
| **TerraPoiNT** | 3D positioning and timing via 5G | In testing, scored highest in Department of Transportation trials |

### Competitive Position
- **165 patents** on 5G PRS (Positioning Reference Signal) extraction technology
- **900 MHz spectrum** covers 90%+ of US population
- **100% software-based** — no custom hardware required
- **Contiguous 12 MHz** of spectrum (post-acquisition)

### The Moat
Their real asset isn't the technology — it's the **spectrum license**. If the FCC approves their petition to reconfigure the Lower 900 MHz band for 5G PNT, NextNav becomes the gatekeeper for a national GPS backup system. That's monopoly economics in critical infrastructure.

## 4. The Catalyst: FCC NPRM
