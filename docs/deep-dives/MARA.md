## 1. Company Overview

MARA Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: MARA) is one of the largest publicly traded Bitcoin mining and digital asset infrastructure companies. Formerly Marathon Digital Holdings, the company rebranded to MARA in 2024 to reflect its broader energy and compute infrastructure ambitions.

**Business Model:**
- **Bitcoin Mining:** Self-mining operations across North America with 1.7 GW of captive power capacity (1.1 GW operational)
- **Energy Infrastructure:** Low-cost power assets and data center sites repurposable for AI/high-performance computing
- **Digital Asset Treasury:** Year-end 2025 holdings of 53,822 BTC (~\$4.7B at quarter-end prices), including 15,315 BTC loaned or pledged as collateral
- **HPC/AI Pivot:** Strategic partnerships (Starwood Capital) to convert select mining sites into AI data centers; still nascent but active

**Competitive Moat:**
- Scale: Among the largest public miners by hashrate (72.2 EH/s energized as of Q1 2026, up 33% YoY from 54.3 EH/s)
- Energy access: 1.7 GW captive capacity with pipeline exceeding 3 GW of low-cost power opportunities
- Operational efficiency: Fleet efficiency of 17.6 J/TH as of Q1 2026
- Balance sheet strength: BTC treasury and convertible-debt reductions provide NAV anchor and leverage to Bitcoin price appreciation

**Management:**
- CEO Fred Thiel (Chairman) — 35+ years tech sector experience, driving AI infrastructure pivot
- Strategy: "Dynamic allocation of power across multiple compute workloads" — mining when BTC profitable, AI/HPC when margins justify

## 5. Risk Factors

**Business Risks:**
- **Single-Asset Concentration:** 90%+ of value tied to Bitcoin price — extreme volatility
- **Mining Margin Compression:** Global hashrate growth (1,031 EH/s average in Sept 2025, +9% MoM) erodes per-miner rewards
- **AI Pivot Execution Risk:** Data center conversion is capital-intensive; competition from dedicated AI infra players
- **Fleet Obsolescence:** ASIC miners depreciate rapidly; continuous capex required

**Financial Risks:**
- **Mark-to-Market Volatility:** Q1 2026 showed how BTC fair-value impairments (~\$1.0B) can overwhelm operating results
- **Convertible Debt Overhang:** Debt fell from ~\$3.3B at end-2025 to ~\$2.3B after Q1 2026 BTC sales, but still significant dilution and refinancing risk
- **Negative FCF During Expansion:** Heavy capex requirements strain liquidity when BTC prices are low; Q1 2026 BTC sale raised \$1.5B to fund operations and debt reduction

**Macro/Regulatory Risks:**
- **Crypto Crackdown:** SEC or global regulatory action against Bitcoin mining (environmental concerns, energy usage)
- **Bitcoin Protocol Risk:** Halving events (next: 2028) cut block rewards by 50%, structurally reducing revenue
- **Energy Price Spikes:** Low-cost power contracts may not be renewable at current rates

## 8. Readability Pass

**What is Bitcoin Mining?**
Miners use specialized computers (ASICs) to solve mathematical puzzles that secure the Bitcoin network. The winner of each puzzle gets a block reward (currently 3.125 BTC, halving to 1.5625 in 2028) plus transaction fees. MARA operates thousands of these machines in data centers powered by cheap electricity.

**What is Hashrate?**
Hashrate measures total computing power directed at Bitcoin mining. Higher hashrate = more competition = harder to win blocks. MARA's 75 EH/s target means 75 quintillion guesses per second.

**What are Convertible Notes?**
Debt that can be converted into stock. MARA's convertible notes have been reduced from ~\$3.3B at end-2025 to ~\$2.3B after the Q1 2026 BTC sale, but any remaining amount still dilutes shareholders if converted. Cheap capital for a growth company, but an overhang if BTC stays low.

**Why is MARA Pivoting to AI?**
Bitcoin mining uses massive amounts of power and computing hardware. When BTC mining is less profitable, MARA can redirect that power and infrastructure to AI data centers — essentially renting out its energy assets to AI companies.

**NAV Premium/Discount?**
NAV = value of BTC holdings / shares outstanding. If MARA trades above NAV, you're paying extra for management and growth. Below NAV, you're buying BTC at a discount through the stock.
