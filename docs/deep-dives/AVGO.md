## 1. Company Overview

Broadcom Inc. (NASDAQ: AVGO) is a global technology leader designing and supplying a broad range of semiconductor and infrastructure software solutions. The company operates through two segments: **Semiconductor Solutions** and **Infrastructure Software**.

- **Semiconductor Solutions** (~70% of revenue): AI ASICs, networking (Ethernet switching, routing), optical interconnects, Wi-Fi / broadband access, storage adapters, and RF components. Broadcom is the dominant merchant supplier of custom AI silicon to hyperscalers, with deep co-design relationships at Google, Meta, and others.
- **Infrastructure Software** (~30% of revenue): Led by VMware, which Broadcom acquired in 2023, this segment provides virtualization, cloud management, and mainframe software. Integration is ongoing and driving margin expansion through product rationalization and subscription transitions.

Broadcom’s moat rests on decades of IP accumulation, deep customer co-design relationships, and the ability to bundle silicon plus software into full-stack data center solutions.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **AI ASIC Ramp:** Broadcom is the leading merchant AI ASIC provider. Custom silicon for Google (TPU v6), Meta (MTIA), and other hyperscalers is entering high-volume production. Each additional customer adds billions in incremental TAM.
2. **Optical Interconnect Upgrades:** Data center bandwidth demands are pushing 800G and 1.6T optical transceiver adoption. Broadcom’s silicon photonics and DSP portfolio is best-in-class and tightly coupled with its switching silicon.
3. **VMware Subscription Transition:** The shift from perpetual licenses to SaaS subscriptions is painful in the short term but should lift recurring revenue visibility and margins by FY2027.
4. **Networking Share Gains:** Broadcom’s Tomahawk 5 / Jericho3 switching and routing silicon is winning sockets in AI back-end networks, displacing merchant and in-house alternatives.
5. **Capital Return:** The $3B buyback and consistent dividend growth support total shareholder returns even if the multiple compresses.

---

## 5. Risk Factors

1. **Expectations Mismatch:** Broadcom dropped >12% after Q2 FY2026 earnings despite record results. The market had priced in even higher AI revenue guidance. Near-term expectations may remain elevated.
2. **Hyperscaler Concentration:** A small number of very large customers (Google, Meta, Microsoft) drive the majority of AI semiconductor revenue. Any capex pause or in-sourcing decision (e.g., Google’s TPU vertical integration) would disproportionately impact Broadcom.
3. **VMware Integration Risk:** Customer churn, product-line consolidation, and sales force disruption could delay software margin recovery.
4. **Competitive Pressure:** Marvell (MRVL) is gaining share in custom AI silicon and networking. NVIDIA’s dominance in training may limit ASIC TAM for certain workloads.
5. **Macro / Cyclicality:** Semiconductor demand is historically cyclical. A broad AI capex down-cycle would pressure all AI infrastructure names, including AVGO.

## 6. Recommendation

**Rating: <span class="rating-buy">BUY</span>**

Broadcom is a core holding for exposure to the AI buildout theme. The combination of custom AI silicon leadership, best-in-class networking/optical interconnects, and a stabilizing software business creates a durable earnings trajectory. The post-earnings pullback has improved the risk/reward setup, with the stock now trading closer to base-case fair value rather than bull-case exuberance.

- **Entry:** Current levels (~$410) or on any weakness toward $380–400.
- **Stop / Risk Management:** A sustained break below $340 (bear-case lower bound) would suggest a structural AI capex slowdown or material VMware deterioration.
- **Time Horizon:** 12–18 months to realize base-case targets; 24–36 months for bull-case AI revenue >$100B to fully price in.

## 7. Readability Pass

This deep dive is written for investors with a working knowledge of semiconductors and AI infrastructure. Key terms:
- **ASIC** = Application-Specific Integrated Circuit (custom chip designed for a specific workload, such as AI inference).
- **Optical Interconnect** = Fiber-optic links between servers and switches in a data center, measured in speeds like 800G or 1.6T (terabits per second).
- **SaaS / Subscription Transition** = Moving customers from one-time software licenses to recurring monthly or annual fees.
- **TPU / MTIA** = Google’s Tensor Processing Unit and Meta’s Training & Inference Accelerator — custom AI chips co-designed with Broadcom.
