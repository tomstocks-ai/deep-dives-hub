# SLV — iShares Silver Trust

## 1. OVERVIEW

iShares Silver Trust (SLV) is the largest physically-backed silver ETF, holding ~13,500 tonnes of silver bullion in London vaults. Each share represents a fractional claim on physical silver. It trades like a stock but tracks the spot price of silver minus a 0.50% annual expense ratio.

**What this means:** SLV is not a company — it is a vault with a ticker. When you buy SLV, you are buying exposure to silver price movements without dealing with futures contracts or physical bars.

## 2. FUNDAMENTAL DRIVERS

### Supply & Demand Dynamics
Silver is unique among precious metals because roughly **50% of demand is industrial** (solar panels, electronics, batteries, medical) and 50% is investment/jewelry. This dual role creates a more complex demand profile than gold.

- **Supply:** Global mine production ~830M oz/year. Recycling adds ~170M oz. Total supply ~1,000M oz.
- **Demand:** Industrial demand ~550M oz (solar is the fastest-growing segment), investment ~300M oz, jewelry ~200M oz.
- **Deficit:** The silver market has been in structural deficit for 5+ consecutive years. Above-ground stockpiles are being drawn down.

**What this means:** More silver is being used than mined. The gap is filled by selling from existing stockpiles. When stockpiles shrink, prices rise.

### Key Demand Drivers

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Solar PV expansion | Silver is essential for photovoltaic cells. Each gigawatt of solar capacity requires ~20 tonnes of silver. Global solar installations growing 15–20% annually. |
| EVs & batteries | Silver use in EV electronics and next-gen batteries (solid-state, sodium-ion). |
| AI data centers | High-performance chips and interconnects use silver for conductivity. |
| Investment demand | ETF flows, coin/bar purchases surge during inflation/geopolitical stress. |
| 5G/IoT | Silver's unmatched electrical conductivity makes it irreplaceable in high-frequency electronics. |

### Silver vs Gold Ratio
The gold/silver ratio currently sits near **80:1** (it takes 80 oz of silver to buy 1 oz of gold). Historical average is ~50:1. If the ratio mean-reverts, silver outperforms gold significantly.

**What this means:** Silver looks cheap relative to gold by historical standards. A normalization of this ratio implies silver prices 40–60% higher even if gold stays flat.

## 3. VALUATION & PRICE TARGETS

Silver is not a cash-flowing asset — valuation is based on supply/demand balance, real interest rates, and the gold/silver ratio.

| Scenario | Price Target | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Bull** | \$75–85/oz | Supply deficit widens; solar demand accelerates; investment demand surges on recession fears; gold/silver ratio compresses to 55:1. |
| **Base** | \$50–60/oz | Steady industrial demand; modest investment flows; Fed cuts rates 1–2x; ratio normalizes to 65:1. |
| **Bear** | \$32–38/oz | Global recession crushes industrial demand; solar silver thrifting reduces intensity; strong dollar persists. |

**Current spot:** ~\$36/oz (SLV ~\$61.57/share)

## 4. CATALYSTS

- **Fed rate cuts** — Lower real rates reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding silver.
- **Solar installation boom** — China, India, and U.S. IRA subsidies driving record PV deployments.
- **Geopolitical shocks** — Middle East, Taiwan, or trade war escalation drives safe-haven buying.
- **Silver thrusting failure** — If solar manufacturers cannot reduce silver usage per cell faster than capacity grows, demand explodes.
- **LBMA inventory drawdown** — Registered stocks in London and COMEX warehouses falling to multi-year lows.

## 5. RISKS

- **Industrial demand collapse** — Recession hits solar, auto, and electronics sectors simultaneously.
- **Silver substitution** — Copper or aluminum replaces silver in some electronics; TOPCon solar cells reduce silver intensity.
- **Strong dollar** — DXY above 110 historically suppresses precious metals.
- **ETF outflows** — SLV holdings have declined before during risk-off periods as investors sell "what they can."
- **Regulatory** — Potential changes to commodity ETF structures or tax treatment.

## 8. INVESTMENT THESIS

### Bull Case
Silver is the most undervalued precious metal. Structural supply deficit + electrification demand (solar, EVs, AI) creates a demand floor. Investment demand spikes during uncertainty. Gold/silver ratio compression adds a kicker. Target \$75–85.

### Base Case
Steady grind higher. Industrial demand grows 3–4% annually. Investment demand is moderate. Fed cuts once or twice. Silver reaches \$50–60 by end of 2026. SLV returns 15–25% from current levels.

### Bear Case
Global manufacturing recession. Solar silver usage falls faster than capacity rises. Dollar strength persists. Silver retests \$30–32. SLV drops 15–20%.

## 10. READABILITY PASS

- **ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund):** A basket of assets (here, physical silver bars) that trades on a stock exchange like a single stock.
- **Spot price:** The current market price for immediate delivery of a commodity.
- **Gold/silver ratio:** How many ounces of silver it takes to buy one ounce of gold. A high number means silver is cheap relative to gold.
- **LBMA:** London Bullion Market Association — the main hub for wholesale silver trading.
- **COT report:** Commitments of Traders — a weekly report showing what different types of traders (producers, speculators) are doing in futures markets.
- **Real interest rates:** The interest rate after subtracting inflation. When real rates are low or negative, holding silver (which pays no interest) becomes more attractive.
