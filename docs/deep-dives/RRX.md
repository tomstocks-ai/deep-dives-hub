# RRX — Regal Rexnord Corporation

Regal Rexnord is a diversified industrial maker of electric motors, power transmission, and automation/motion-control products that has quietly become an AI data-center beneficiary. Its Automation & Motion Control segment is booming on data-center power management and precision-motion demand — Q4 2025 brought roughly \$735M of data-center orders and Q1 2026 AMC orders grew over 34%. FY2025 revenue was roughly flat at \$5.93B, but adjusted EPS reached \$9.65 with \$893M of adjusted free cash flow driving rapid deleveraging, and management guides FY2026 to \$10.20–\$11.00 adjusted EPS. Crucially, unlike most AI-adjacent names, RRX is not expensive: at ~\$207 it trades at only ~18–19x forward earnings with a Strong Buy consensus and a ~\$260 target (~26% upside). Reasonable valuation, a real catalyst, margin expansion, and deleveraging optionality make this a **BUY**.

## 1. Company Overview

**Regal Rexnord Corporation** (NYSE: RRX), headquartered in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, engineers and manufactures electric motors, power transmission components, and automation/motion-control subsystems. Formed through the 2021 merger of Regal Beloit and Rexnord's Process & Motion Control business (and bolstered by the Altra Industrial Motion acquisition), the company sells the motors, gearing, bearings, conveyors, and precision-motion products that move and control industrial equipment across dozens of end markets.

### Business Model and Revenue Segments

Regal Rexnord reports in three segments:

| Segment | Description | Role |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Industrial Powertrain Solutions (IPS)** | Gearing, bearings, couplings, clutches, brakes, and conveying — the mechanical powertrain | Largest revenue segment; core cash generator |
| **Automation & Motion Control (AMC)** | Conveyor and conveying automation subsystems, aerospace components, precision motion, servo motors, drives, linear actuators, and power management | The growth and AI/data-center engine (orders +34% in Q1 2026) |
| **Power Efficiency Solutions (PES)** | Electric motors and controls for HVAC, pool/pump, and general commercial/residential applications | Shorter-cycle, efficiency-driven |

The strategic story is a mix shift toward **higher-margin automation and data-center content**, plus cross-selling integrated "powertrain + automation" solutions. Data centers have emerged as a major demand pool for AMC's power-management and precision-motion products.

### Competitive Moat

- **Breadth and installed base:** One of the broadest portfolios of motors, powertrain, and motion products, deeply specified into customer equipment with long lifecycles and aftermarket pull-through.
- **Mission-critical components:** Motors and powertrain parts are essential, engineered-in content where reliability matters more than price, supporting share and pricing.
- **Margin self-help:** Gross margin has climbed from roughly 26% to ~38% over seven years via portfolio upgrades, synergies, and "80/20" simplification — a structural profitability improvement.
- **Data-center positioning:** AMC's power-management and precision-motion products are winning meaningful data-center orders, tying a legacy industrial to the AI buildout.

### Management

Longtime CEO **Louis Pinkham** led the merger integration and margin transformation; **Aamir Paul** took over as CEO around July 1, 2026, tasked with sustaining the growth-and-margin playbook. CFO **Robert Rehard** has guided a disciplined deleveraging and capital-allocation plan. The leadership transition is a watch item but comes with continuity of strategy.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **AI data-center demand (the new engine):** Data centers have become a major AMC demand pool for power-management and precision-motion products. Regal Rexnord booked ~\$735M of data-center orders in Q4 2025, and AMC orders grew over 34% in Q1 2026. Conversion of these orders into revenue is the central near-term catalyst.

2. **Order and backlog momentum:** Full-year 2025 daily orders rose 15.5%, Q4 orders jumped 53.8%, and Q1 2026 backlog was up 6.7% sequentially — pointing to a revenue re-acceleration after a soft 2025.

3. **Margin expansion:** Gross margin has climbed from ~26% to ~38% over seven years, and FY2026 adjusted EBITDA margin is guided to 22.2%. Continued mix shift toward automation and "80/20" simplification support further gains.

4. **Deleveraging into capital returns:** Strong free cash flow (\$893M adjusted in 2025, ~\$650M guided for 2026) is cutting net debt/EBITDA from ~3.1x toward ~2.7x, after which capital allocation is expected to pivot to buybacks and bolt-on M&A.

5. **Tariff mitigation and self-help:** Management cut unmitigated tariff exposure to \$127M (from \$155M) and expects to be dollar-cost neutral by mid-2026 and margin-neutral by year-end, removing an overhang.

## 5. Risk Factors

### Business Risks

- **Order-to-revenue conversion:** The bull case depends on strong data-center and automation orders converting to revenue and margin as guided; organic growth was still only +1.6% in Q1 2026.
- **Cyclicality:** Motors, powertrain, and industrial automation demand tracks manufacturing and capex cycles, and several end markets remain soft.
- **Execution and CEO transition:** A new CEO (Aamir Paul) inherits a complex, recently merged portfolio; sustaining the margin trajectory is not guaranteed.

### Financial Risks

- **Elevated leverage (the key risk):** Net debt/adjusted EBITDA near 3.1x amplifies downside if the cycle turns, though it is declining. This is the main reason to size the position with care.
- **Tariffs and mix:** Despite mitigation, tariffs, input costs, and unfavorable mix could pressure the 22.2% EBITDA margin target.

### Macro and Sector Risks

- **Industrials de-rating:** The stock is up ~51% in 2026, so a broad industrials pullback or risk-off rotation would hit it even with solid fundamentals.
- **Data-center capex cycle:** A slowdown in AI/data-center construction would remove the incremental growth driver.
- **FX and global demand:** Global operations carry currency and demand-cycle exposure across regions.

**Rating: <span class="rating-buy">BUY</span>**

Regal Rexnord is the rare AI-adjacent industrial that is not priced for perfection. The business pairs a broad, mission-critical portfolio of motors, powertrain, and automation products with a genuine new growth engine — data-center demand driving AMC orders up more than 34% and ~\$735M of data-center bookings — on top of a multi-year margin-expansion story (gross margin from ~26% to ~38%) and strong free cash flow that is rapidly deleveraging the balance sheet. Management guides FY2026 to ~10% adjusted EPS growth (\$10.20–\$11.00).

## 8. Readability and Clarity Pass

**What Regal Rexnord actually does:** Regal Rexnord makes the motors, gears, bearings, and motion-control parts that move and power industrial machines — everything from factory conveyors and pumps to precision robotics and aerospace actuators. Recently, it has found a fast-growing new customer base: data centers, which need its power-management and precision-motion products. That has connected an old-economy industrial to the AI boom.

**Why the stock is interesting:** Orders are surging (data-center orders alone were ~\$735M in one quarter), profit margins have improved a lot over the years, and the company is paying down debt with strong cash flow. And unlike most "AI" stocks, it isn't expensive — about 18–19x next year's expected earnings — while Wall Street sees roughly 26% upside. The main risks are its debt load and the fact that industrial demand rises and falls with the economy.

**Key terms explained:**

- **Three segments:** *Industrial Powertrain Solutions (IPS)* — mechanical parts like gears and bearings (biggest); *Automation & Motion Control (AMC)* — automation and precision motion, the data-center growth engine; *Power Efficiency Solutions (PES)* — electric motors for HVAC and pumps.
- **Orders / backlog:** New orders and the unfilled order pile — leading indicators of future revenue. RRX's are rising fast.
- **Adjusted vs GAAP EPS:** GAAP includes big non-cash merger costs (making the P/E look high); adjusted strips them out (\$9.65 in 2025), and is what management guides on.
- **Net debt / EBITDA (leverage):** How much debt the company carries relative to earnings. ~3.1x is elevated but falling toward ~2.7x.
- **Free cash flow:** Cash left after running the business (\$893M in 2025) — used here to cut debt and, increasingly, to buy back stock.
