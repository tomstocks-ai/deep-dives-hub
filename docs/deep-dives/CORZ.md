## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Core Scientific, Inc. (NASDAQ: CORZ) is a digital infrastructure company that operates large-scale data centers for bitcoin mining and high-density colocation (HDC) services for AI and high-performance computing (HPC) workloads. The company is executing a strategic pivot from low-margin self-mining to higher-margin infrastructure hosting.

**Business Model and Revenue Segments:**

- **Digital Asset Mining:** Self-mining bitcoin using owned ASIC miners. Revenue tied to BTC price, network difficulty, and hash rate. This segment is declining as the company reallocates capacity to colocation.
- **High-Density Colocation (HDC):** Hosting third-party AI/HPC infrastructure in Core Scientific's data centers. This is the growth engine — higher margins, long-term contracts, and less exposure to BTC volatility.
- **Equipment Sales:** Occasional sales of mining equipment.

Revenue mix is shifting rapidly. HDC revenue grew 168% YoY in FY2025 to \$65.4M, while total revenue declined 38% to \$319M as mining revenue shrank.

**Industry Position and Competitive Moat:**

- One of the largest publicly traded bitcoin miners in North America by energized hash rate (~19 EH/s).
- ~745 MW of total power capacity across facilities in Texas, North Carolina, Oklahoma, and Kentucky.
- Existing relationships with hyperscalers (CoreWeave is a major HDC customer) provide a pathway to AI infrastructure hosting.
- Moat is operational scale and power access, not technology. The pivot to HDC is replicable by other miners with similar power portfolios.

**Management and Ownership:**

- CEO Adam Sullivan has led the strategic pivot since 2022.
- The company emerged from Chapter 11 bankruptcy in January 2024, giving management a fresh balance sheet — but also a history of financial distress.
- Insider ownership is moderate; institutional ownership is high post-restructuring.

**Key Facilities and Jurisdictions:**

- Primary operations in Texas (Denton, Midland), North Carolina (Lenoir), Oklahoma, and Kentucky.
- Power contracts are a mix of fixed and variable-rate agreements; Texas exposure means ERCOT pricing volatility.

**Balance Sheet Notes:**

- Heavy capex commitments for HDC buildout. Q3 2025 capex was \$244.5M, of which \$196.4M was funded by CoreWeave under existing agreements.

### Cash Flow

- **Operating Cash Flow:** Positive but volatile. Mining generates cash when BTC prices are high; HDC contracts provide more stable cash flows.
- **Free Cash Flow:** Negative due to heavy capex. The HDC transition requires significant upfront investment in data center upgrades (power, cooling, rack density).
- **Cash Burn:** The company has ~\$530M in liquidity. At the current capex run rate, this provides roughly 2–3 quarters of funding without additional capital raises — but CoreWeave-funded capex reduces the net cash burn.
- **Capital Intensity:** Very high. Data center infrastructure and mining equipment are capital-intensive. The pivot to HDC requires even more capex (higher power density, liquid cooling, etc.).

## 3. VALUATION

### Multiples and Metrics

Core Scientific is not profitable on an operating basis, so traditional P/E and EV/EBITDA multiples are not meaningful. The stock trades on a sum-of-the-parts (SOTP) narrative: mining assets + HDC growth optionality.

| Metric | Value | Context |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Market Cap | ~\$8.9B | At \$28.18/share, ~315M shares outstanding |
| P/S (FY2025) | ~28x | Extremely high for a declining-revenue business |
| EV/Revenue | ~30x+ | Reflects HDC growth optionality, not current fundamentals |
| Price/Book | N/A | Negative book value |

**Valuation Reality:** The stock is priced for a successful HDC pivot. At ~28x sales, the market is assuming HDC revenue will grow to hundreds of millions annually with high margins. Any execution miss will compress the multiple rapidly.

### DCF / Scenario Analysis

Given the binary nature of the HDC pivot and the lack of operating profitability, a traditional DCF is unreliable. Instead, we use a sum-of-the-parts approach with scenario-based price targets.

**Assumptions:**

- Mining business: valued at 1–2x revenue (declining, low-margin).
- HDC business: valued at 8–15x revenue (growth, higher-margin, comparable to data center REITs and AI infrastructure peers).
- FY2026 revenue guidance: Not explicitly provided, but HDC revenue could reach \$150–200M if growth continues; mining revenue likely flat-to-down at ~\$250M.
- Total FY2026 revenue estimate: \$400–450M (vs. \$319M in FY2025).

| Scenario | Probability | Implied Value | Price Target |
|----------|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Bull** | 25% | HDC scales to \$300M+ by 2027; mining stabilizes; company achieves EBITDA profitability. SOTP: mining \$400M + HDC \$4.5B + cash = \$5.4B equity → \$38–42/share | **\$38–42** |
| **Base** | 50% | HDC grows to \$150–200M by 2027; mining flat; operating losses narrow but persist. SOTP: mining \$300M + HDC \$2.0B + cash = \$2.5B equity → \$22–26/share | **\$22–26** |
| **Bear** | 25% | HDC growth stalls; mining revenue collapses further; cash burn forces dilutive raise. SOTP: mining \$150M + HDC \$500M + cash = \$1.0B equity → \$12–16/share | **\$12–16** |

**Implied Growth Rate at Current Price:** The market is pricing in ~\$500M+ HDC revenue by 2027–2028 at 15x+ multiples. This is aggressive but not impossible if AI demand for data center capacity remains strong.

## 4. GROWTH CATALYSTS

- **HDC Revenue Acceleration:** The company is converting existing mining facilities to support AI/HPC workloads. If HDC revenue grows from \$65M in FY2025 to \$150–200M in FY2026, the narrative shifts from "declining miner" to "AI infrastructure play."
- **New AI Customer Wins:** CoreWeave is the anchor HDC customer. Landing additional hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta) would validate the infrastructure and drive contract visibility.
- **Block 3nm ASIC Deployment:** The company has a deal with Block for next-generation 3nm ASIC miners, adding ~15 EH/s of future capacity. This provides mining optionality if BTC prices rise.
- **Bitcoin Price Appreciation:** A BTC bull market would improve mining cash flows and reduce the urgency of the HDC pivot. The company holds minimal BTC on its balance sheet (sells most production), so upside is indirect.
- **Potential M&A or Partnerships:** The failed CoreWeave merger demonstrated strategic interest in Core Scientific's assets. Other AI infrastructure players or data center REITs could pursue partnerships or acquisitions.
- **Regulatory Tailwinds:** Pro-crypto regulatory stance in the U.S. could reduce operational risks for mining and improve access to power contracts.

## 5. RISK FACTORS

### Business Risks
- **Customer Concentration:** CoreWeave is the dominant HDC customer. Losing CoreWeave or a contract renegotiation would devastate the HDC narrative.
- **Execution Risk on HDC Pivot:** Converting mining facilities to HDC is not trivial. It requires power upgrades, cooling retrofits, and higher-density rack configurations. Delays or cost overruns are likely.
- **Declining Mining Revenue:** The core business is shrinking. If the HDC pivot does not scale fast enough, total revenue will continue to decline, and the stock will re-rate downward.
- **Technology Obsolescence:** Mining ASICs depreciate rapidly. The Block 3nm deal is promising but unproven.

### Financial Risks
- **Negative Equity and Balance Sheet Fragility:** The company has negative book value. A downturn in operations or a failed capital raise could trigger covenant issues or liquidity crises.
- **Heavy Cash Burn and Capex Requirements:** The HDC pivot requires hundreds of millions in capex. If CoreWeave stops funding capex or if the company cannot access capital markets, the transition stalls.
- **Dilution Risk:** Warrant overhang and potential future equity raises could dilute existing shareholders significantly.
- **Material Weakness in Internal Controls:** The restatement and disclosed control weakness raise governance concerns and increase the risk of future accounting surprises.

### Macro and Sector Risks
- **Bitcoin Price Volatility:** Mining revenue and cash flow are directly tied to BTC price. A sustained bear market in BTC would strain liquidity.
- **Regulatory Risk:** Mining faces environmental scrutiny (energy consumption, carbon footprint). HDC faces less regulatory risk but is not immune.
- **Power Price Volatility:** Texas ERCOT pricing can spike during extreme weather, increasing operating costs.
- **Competition:** Other bitcoin miners (Riot, Marathon, CleanSpark) are also pivoting to HDC. The competitive moat is thin.
- **ESG Considerations:** Bitcoin mining's energy intensity creates ESG headwinds that may limit institutional investment.

## 7. READABILITY AND CLARITY PASS

**What is "hash rate"?** Think of it as the total computing power a miner deploys to solve bitcoin's mathematical puzzles. More hash rate = more chances to earn bitcoin rewards. Core Scientific operates ~19 EH/s (exahashes per second), which is roughly 19 quintillion guesses per second.

**What are "warrant liabilities"?** When Core Scientific emerged from bankruptcy, it issued warrants (options to buy stock at a fixed price) to creditors and investors. Accounting rules require the company to record these as a liability on the balance sheet, even though they are not debt. The liability fluctuates with the stock price — when CORZ rises, the warrant liability increases, creating a non-cash "loss." When CORZ falls, the liability decreases, creating a non-cash "gain." These accounting swings distort net income but do not affect cash flow.

**What is "HDC" (High-Density Colocation)?** Instead of mining bitcoin for itself, Core Scientific rents out its data center space to companies that need to run AI and supercomputing equipment. AI servers consume enormous power and generate massive heat, so they need specialized facilities. HDC is higher-margin and more stable than mining because it generates recurring rental income rather than volatile bitcoin revenue.

**What does "negative book value" mean?** On paper, Core Scientific's liabilities exceed its assets. This sounds alarming, but the "liabilities" include \$1.3B of warrant liabilities that are not real debt. The company has \$530M in cash and generates positive operating cash flow, so it is not in immediate danger of bankruptcy. However, the balance sheet is weak, and the company cannot afford a major operational setback.

**What is the "Block 3nm ASIC deal"?** Block (formerly Square, led by Jack Dorsey) is designing a new generation of bitcoin mining chips using 3-nanometer technology — the most advanced chip manufacturing process. Core Scientific has a deal to buy these chips, which would add ~15 EH/s of mining capacity. This is a future option: if bitcoin prices rise, the new chips will be highly profitable. If prices fall, the company can delay deployment.
