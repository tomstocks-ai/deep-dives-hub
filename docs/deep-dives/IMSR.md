## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

### Business Model
Terrestrial Energy Inc. is an advanced nuclear technology company developing the **Integral Molten Salt Reactor (IMSR)**—a Generation IV ("Gen IV") small modular reactor (SMR) designed to deliver zero-carbon, reliable electricity and industrial heat at competitive costs.

**Key Differentiator:** Unlike competitors using High-Assay Low Enriched Uranium (HALEU), IMSR uses **Standard-Assay Low Enriched Uranium (SALEU)**—the same fuel used by conventional nuclear plants for decades. This eliminates supply chain bottlenecks and regulatory uncertainty facing HALEU-dependent competitors.

### Revenue Model (Pre-Revenue Stage)
The company expects **four principal revenue streams** once commercial operations begin (targeting early 2030s):

| Revenue Stream | % of Lifecycle Revenue | Description |
|----------------|------------------------|-------------|
| Pre-construction services | ~4% | Site assessment, engineering, permit preparation |
| Construction & component supply | ~23% | IMSR Core-units, fuel salt, project management |
| Post-construction Core-unit supply | ~55% | Recurring replacement every 7 years (16 units over 56-year life) |
| Post-construction fuel supply | ~18% | Annual fuel salt supply + O&M services |

**What this means:** Unlike typical development-stage companies, IMSR is designing a **60+ year recurring revenue model** where each plant generates predictable cash flows through core replacements and fuel supply—similar to a "razor-and-blades" model with extremely high customer retention.

### Competitive Moat

1. **First-Mover in Regulatory Approval:**
   - First molten salt reactor to complete Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission (CNSC) Vendor Design Review (April 2023)
   - First NRC Safety Evaluation for molten salt reactor approved (September 2025)
   - Only private company signatory to Gen IV International Forum's MSR System Steering Committee

2. **Fuel Advantage:**
   - SALEU fuel avoids HALEU supply chain crisis affecting competitors
   - No reliance on Russian uranium enrichment (geopolitical risk mitigation)

3. **Technology Differentiation:**
   - Operates at **585°C**—higher than legacy nuclear (<300°C) and enabling industrial heat applications
   - **Black-start capability** (can restart without grid power)
   - **Load-following** (can adjust output quickly to back up renewables)

4. **Project Pipeline:**
   - 10+ early-stage projects in development
   - 50+ collaborative industry relationships
   - Strategic partnerships with Schneider Electric, Zachry Group, Texas A&M, Ameresco

## 11. READABILITY & CLARITY PASS

### Key Terms Explained

**Molten Salt Reactor (MSR):** A nuclear reactor that uses liquid salt (instead of water) as both fuel and coolant. Think of it like a nuclear-powered lava lamp—the liquid fuel circulates through the reactor, generating heat that can be used for electricity or industrial processes.

**Generation IV (Gen IV):** The next generation of nuclear reactors designed to be safer, more efficient, and more versatile than current (Gen III) reactors. IMSR is one of several Gen IV technologies being developed.

**SALEU vs. HALEU (Nuclear Fuel):**
- **SALEU (Standard-Assay Low Enriched Uranium):** Uranium enriched to <5%—the standard fuel for today's nuclear plants. Widely available.
- **HALEU (High-Assay Low Enriched Uranium):** Uranium enriched to 15-20%—required by many advanced reactors. Currently scarce and expensive.

**What this means:** IMSR's use of SALEU is like using regular gasoline instead of racing fuel—easier to find, cheaper, and more familiar to regulators.

**SPAC (Special Purpose Acquisition Company):** A "blank check" company that raises money from investors with the sole purpose of merging with a private company to take it public. IMSR went public by merging with HCM II Acquisition Corp.

**Vendor Design Review (VDR):** A pre-licensing review where regulators examine a nuclear design to identify any "showstopper" issues before a company spends billions building it. Think of it as an architect's plan review before construction begins.

**Core-Unit Replacement:** The IMSR reactor core is designed to be swapped out every 7 years like a battery. This creates recurring revenue for Terrestrial Energy throughout the plant's 56-year life.

### Financial Metrics in Plain Language

**Market Cap ($661M):** The total value of all Terrestrial Energy shares combined. If you wanted to buy the entire company, this is what you'd theoretically pay.

**Enterprise Value ($353M):** Market cap minus cash. This represents the value of the actual business operations, separate from the cash sitting in the bank.

**Accumulated Deficit ($124.6M):** The total amount of money the company has lost since it was founded. This is common for development-stage companies.

**Cash Burn Rate (~$23M/year):** How fast the company is spending its cash. With $27.7M in the bank, they'd run out in just over a year without the SPAC proceeds.

**Beta (0.47):** How much the stock moves relative to the overall market. A beta below 1 means the stock is less volatile than the market.

### Investment Concepts Explained

**Pre-Revenue Company:** A company that hasn't started selling its product yet. All of IMSR's current "revenue" is from engineering studies and early development work—not from actual reactor sales.

**Development-Stage Risk:** The risk that a company won't successfully develop its product. For IMSR, this means the risk that they never get regulatory approval or that their reactor design doesn't work as planned.

**Dilution Risk:** When a company issues new shares to raise money, existing shareholders own a smaller percentage of the company. This "dilutes" their ownership.

**Recurring Revenue:** Revenue that comes in regularly rather than as one-time sales. IMSR's model is designed to generate recurring revenue through fuel supply and core replacements.

**Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE):** The average cost of electricity over a power plant's lifetime. IMSR estimates $69/MWh—competitive with natural gas and cheaper than many renewables when storage is included.

### Summary for Non-Technical Investors

**What Terrestrial Energy Does:** They are designing a new type of small nuclear reactor that uses molten salt instead of water. Their reactor is safer, more versatile, and can be built smaller and cheaper than traditional nuclear plants.

**The Opportunity:** The world needs clean, reliable energy. Nuclear is experiencing a renaissance, and small modular reactors are seen as the future. IMSR has a differentiated technology and is ahead of competitors on regulatory approvals.

**The Risk:** The company is years away from revenue. They need to complete testing, get final regulatory approvals, build their first plant, and prove the economics work. Many things could go wrong along the way.

**The Investment Case:** At current prices ($6.24), investors are paying $661M for a company that could be worth billions if successful—or zero if they fail. This is a high-risk, high-reward bet on the future of nuclear energy.
