## Gists

### SLV — iShares Silver Trust · <span class="rating-buy">BUY</span> · \$61.57

Largest physically-backed silver ETF holding ~13,500 tonnes of silver bullion. Silver is in a 5+ year structural supply deficit with industrial demand (solar, EVs, AI chips) growing faster than mine supply. Gold/silver ratio near 80:1 is historically elevated — mean reversion to 55:1 implies 40–60% upside even if gold is flat. RSI at 35.19 near oversold after pullback from \$80 highs.

**Bull:** \$75–85/oz · **Base:** \$50–60/oz · **Bear:** \$32–38/oz

### GLD — SPDR Gold Shares · <span class="rating-buy">BUY</span> · \$396.24

World's largest physically-backed gold ETF holding ~850 tonnes of gold. Gold is in a structural bull market driven by record central bank buying (\>1,000 tonnes/year), fiscal debasement concerns, and geopolitical fragmentation. J.P. Morgan targets \$5,055 by Q4 2026. Fed rate cuts reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold. RSI at 33.02 near oversold after pullback from all-time highs.

### COPX — Global X Copper Miners ETF · <span class="rating-buy">BUY</span> · \$80.64

Exposure to global copper miners (Freeport-McMoRan, Southern Copper, Antofagasta). Copper faces a structural supply deficit as electrification demand — EVs (3–4x more copper than ICE), renewables, AI data centers, grid modernization — outpaces constrained mine supply. S\&P Global projects 19M tonne shortfall by 2050. Miners amplify copper price moves via operating leverage. Goldman Sachs sees deficit emerging in 2026.

**Bull:** \$110–125 · **Base:** \$90–100 · **Bear:** \$60–70

### URA — Global X Uranium ETF · <span class="rating-buy">BUY</span> · \$45.31

Exposure to global uranium miners (Cameco, Kazatomprom, NexGen, Denison). Nuclear power is experiencing a global renaissance — China building 25+ reactors, Japan restarting 14, U.S. SMR programs advancing. Uranium demand (~204M lbs) already exceeds mine supply (~173M lbs), creating a widening deficit. Industry consensus says \$100+/lb is needed to incentivize new mines. Citi base case \$100/lb by end of 2026.

**Bull:** \$70–85 · **Base:** \$55–65 · **Bear:** \$30–38
