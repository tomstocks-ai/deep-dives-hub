## 1. Executive Summary

BlackBerry Limited (NYSE: BB) has transformed from a iconic consumer handset brand into a dual-platform enterprise software company focused on cybersecurity and IoT/embedded systems. The company operates two core segments: **Secure Communications** (Cylance endpoint security and unified endpoint management) and **QNX** (automotive and industrial embedded operating systems). After years of losses, BlackBerry returned to profitability in Q1 FY2026 — its first profitable quarter since 2022 — driven by 70% gross margins in Secure Communications and disciplined cost management.

FY2026 revenue guidance of \$508–\$538M was raised at the lower end, with adjusted EBITDA expected at \$72–\$87M. QNX revenue is guided to \$250–\$270M and Secure Communications to \$234–\$244M. Management also executed \$10M in share buybacks during Q1, signaling confidence in the turnaround trajectory.

The SPEC. BUY rating reflects a positive cybersecurity pivot with improving fundamentals, balanced by execution risk on QNX growth and the lingering overhang from legacy handset-related revenue declines.

---

## 2. Business Model & Segments

### Secure Communications (Cylance + UEM)
- **Products:** AI-driven endpoint protection (Cylance), unified endpoint management (UEM), and secure communications suite for enterprise and government customers.
- **Q1 FY2026:** Revenue of \$59.5M, adjusted EBITDA of \$9.6M, gross margin of **70%**.
- **Growth drivers:** Cross-sell of Cylance AI into the existing UEM install base, government/defense contract wins, and expansion into zero-trust architecture.

### QNX (IoT / Embedded OS)
- **Products:** Real-time operating system (RTOS) and middleware for automotive, industrial automation, and medical devices.
- **Position:** Dominant embedded OS in automotive; growing design wins in software-defined vehicles (SDV), digital cockpits, and ADAS platforms.
- **Revenue model:** Royalty-per-unit and licensing fees tied to vehicle production volumes and new platform adoption.

### Legacy / Other
- Includes declining patent licensing revenue from the historic handset portfolio and non-core assets. Management continues to evaluate monetization options.

## 4. Investment Thesis

**Bull Case (\$14–\$16):**
- QNX accelerates design wins in next-generation SDV and ADAS platforms, driving royalty growth above guidance.
- Secure Communications successfully cross-sells Cylance AI into the UEM base, lifting ARPU and net revenue retention.
- Government cybersecurity contracts provide stable, multi-year revenue streams.
- Legacy patent portfolio is monetized or divested, unlocking shareholder value.
- Sustained profitability and operating leverage attract institutional capital, re-rating the stock toward SaaS peers.

**Base Case (\$10–\$12):**
- Both segments execute in line with guidance; QNX grows modestly with automotive cycle recovery.
- Secure Communications maintains 70% gross margins and low-teens EBITDA margins.
- The company delivers on its FY2026 EBITDA guidance, proving the turnaround is durable.
- Valuation expands to 3–4x EV/Revenue, consistent with small-cap cybersecurity peers.

**Bear Case (\$5–\$7):**
- QNX automotive adoption slows due to OEM budget cuts or competitive pressure from Linux-based alternatives.
- Secure Communications faces pricing pressure from CrowdStrike, Microsoft, and Palo Alto Networks.
- Legacy revenue declines faster than expected, creating a revenue gap.
- Execution missteps delay profitability, forcing dilutive financing or asset sales.

## 5. Catalysts

1. **QNX Design Wins:** New platform announcements with major OEMs for SDV, digital cockpit, and ADAS.
2. **Secure Communications Cross-Sell:** Cylance AI attach rate improvements within the UEM customer base.
3. **Government Contracts:** Defense and federal cybersecurity awards, particularly in zero-trust and secure communications.
4. **Sustained Profitability:** Demonstrating consecutive quarters of positive adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow.
5. **Patent Portfolio Monetization:** Strategic sale or licensing deal for legacy handset patents.

---

## 6. Risks

1. **QNX Growth Underperformance:** Automotive production cuts or slower SDV adoption could weigh on royalty revenue.
2. **Legacy Revenue Decline:** Handset patent licensing continues to erode, creating a headwind to total revenue.
3. **Competitive Intensity:** Endpoint security is a crowded market dominated by CrowdStrike, Microsoft, and Palo Alto Networks.
4. **Execution Risk:** Integrating Cylance AI across the full Secure Communications stack remains a work in progress.
5. **Liquidity / Volatility:** Small-cap structure and turnaround narrative can lead to elevated volatility.
