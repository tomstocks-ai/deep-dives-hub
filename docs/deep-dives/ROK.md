# ROK — Rockwell Automation, Inc.

Rockwell Automation is the world's largest company dedicated purely to industrial automation and digital transformation — and an increasingly important supplier of the automation that builds and runs AI-era infrastructure like data centers, semiconductor fabs, and automated logistics. After a soft FY2025 (revenue +1% to \$8.34B) as customers digested prior over-ordering, growth has re-accelerated: the latest quarter grew ~12%, management raised FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance to \$12.50–\$13.10, and margins are expanding across all three segments. The franchise is excellent and the moat is wide, but at ~\$462 the stock trades near 48x trailing and ~33x forward earnings and sits close to 52-week highs — a full price for a still-cyclical business. A **HOLD**.

## 1. Company Overview

**Rockwell Automation, Inc.** (NYSE: ROK), headquartered in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, is the largest pure-play industrial-automation company in the world, with roughly 26,000 employees and operations across North America, EMEA, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America. Rockwell helps manufacturers design, operate, and optimize their production — from the controllers, drives, and sensors on the factory floor to the software that models, monitors, and secures the entire operation.

### Business Model and Revenue Segments

Rockwell reports in three segments:

| Segment | Description | FY2025 Segment Margin | Trend |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|-------|
| **Intelligent Devices** | Drives, motion, safety, sensing, industrial components, configured-to-order hardware | 17.3% (+240 bps YoY) | Q4 sales +15% to \$1.086B |
| **Software & Control** | Logix control platforms, visualization, FactoryTalk software, digital twin, Plex/MES | 31.2% (+610 bps YoY) | Q4 sales +31% to \$657M — the margin and growth engine |
| **Lifecycle Services** | Consulting, systems integration, cybersecurity, maintenance, digital services | 14.1% (+160 bps YoY) | Q4 sales -3% to \$573M — process-market exposure |

Total-company segment margin reached **20.7% in FY2025, up 360 bps YoY** — the clearest evidence that Rockwell's cost actions and mix shift toward software are working. The **Logix** control architecture and the **FactoryTalk** software suite sit at the center of the model, increasingly sold with recurring/annual-recurring revenue attached.

### Competitive Moat

- **Installed base and switching costs:** Rockwell's controllers and the Logix ecosystem are embedded in tens of thousands of plants. Ripping out the control layer of a running factory is expensive and risky, which makes the installed base sticky and the aftermarket lucrative.
- **North American leadership:** Rockwell is the clear discrete-automation leader in North America, positioning it as a prime beneficiary of US manufacturing reshoring and a \$2B US investment plan.
- **Software and recurring revenue:** FactoryTalk, Plex (MES), and cybersecurity (SecureOT) are shifting the mix toward higher-margin, stickier software and services — the reason Software & Control margins jumped to 31.2%.
- **Domain depth:** Decades of process and discrete expertise across autos, food and beverage, life sciences, energy, and now data centers and semiconductors, which competitors cannot quickly replicate.

### Management

Chairman and CEO **Blake Moret** has led Rockwell since 2016, steering it through the pandemic supply shock, the 2024–2025 destocking downturn, and a strategic push into software, AI, and recurring revenue. Management has focused on margin expansion, portfolio simplification, and disciplined capital return (dividend plus a fresh \$1B buyback), while positioning Rockwell for the automation of AI-era infrastructure.

## 4. Growth Catalysts

1. **Automation of AI-era infrastructure:** Data centers, semiconductor fabs, and automated logistics/warehousing are new, fast-growing demand pools for Rockwell's drives, controls, and software. Management has repeatedly flagged data-center and semiconductor automation as a key growth driver, and the recent Aalo Atomics win (control systems for the Aalo-X test reactor) shows expansion into advanced-nuclear/SMR construction.

2. **US reshoring and the \$2B investment plan:** As North America's discrete-automation leader, Rockwell is a direct beneficiary of manufacturing reshoring, supported by a \$2B US investment plan focused on domestic operations and digital infrastructure.

3. **Software and recurring revenue:** FactoryTalk (including new FactoryTalk Orchestration and ResilientEdge launches), Plex MES, and SecureOT cybersecurity are shifting the mix toward higher-margin, stickier annual-recurring revenue — the engine behind Software & Control's 31.2% margin.

4. **Margin expansion and cost discipline:** Total segment margin expanded 360 bps in FY2025. Continued cost control, pricing agility, and mix shift toward software provide a path to further margin gains and double-digit EPS growth.

5. **Capital return:** The fresh \$1B buyback plus a ~1.2% dividend yield support EPS growth and total return, and management retains capacity for bolt-on software/services acquisitions.

## 5. Risk Factors

### Business Risks

- **Cyclicality:** Industrial-automation capex is tied to manufacturing cycles and can swing sharply. The FY2024–2025 destocking downturn — where revenue went flat — is a recent reminder that demand is not linear.
- **Lumpy new end markets:** Data-center and semiconductor automation is promising but project-timing dependent and potentially lower-margin or more competitive than Rockwell's core discrete business.
- **Competition:** Siemens, Schneider Electric, Emerson, ABB, and Honeywell all compete across automation hardware, software, and process end markets, and several are larger and more diversified.

### Financial Risks

- **Full valuation (the dominant risk):** At ~48x trailing and ~33x forward earnings, above the historical mid-20s and near 52-week highs, ROK is priced for continued execution. Multiple compression is the primary downside driver, and the ~\$471 consensus is barely above the current price.
- **Margin give-back:** Tariffs, input-cost inflation, and pricing normalization could reverse some of the hard-won margin expansion.

### Macro and Sector Risks

- **Recession sensitivity:** With a ~1.5 beta and a premium multiple, ROK is exposed to a downturn in global manufacturing or a broad industrials de-rating.
- **Geographic and trade exposure:** Global operations carry FX, tariff, and supply-chain risk; a trade shock or slowdown in China/EMEA would weigh on results.
- **Interest rates:** A higher-multiple equity is more sensitive to rising real rates.

## 6. Recommendation

**Rating: <span class="rating-hold">HOLD</span>**

Rockwell Automation is a high-quality, wide-moat franchise at the center of powerful secular themes — factory reshoring, digital transformation, and the automation of AI-era infrastructure. The FY2025 trough is behind it, growth has re-accelerated into the double digits, segment margins expanded 360 bps, management raised FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance to \$12.50–\$13.10, and capital return is disciplined. This is a business worth owning at the right price.

The issue is that the price already reflects much of the good news. At ~\$462 the stock trades near 48x trailing and ~33x forward earnings, well above its historical range, and sits close to its 52-week high with an analyst consensus target (~\$471) only about 2% higher. The base case offers modest upside while the bear case — a cyclical stumble plus multiple compression toward the mid-20s — sits ~15–25% lower. That skew, on a still-cyclical industrial, is why this is a **HOLD** rather than a BUY, despite a franchise I rate highly.

## 8. Readability and Clarity Pass

**What Rockwell actually does:** Rockwell makes the "brains and muscles" of factories. Its controllers, drives, and sensors physically run production lines, and its software (FactoryTalk, Plex) lets manufacturers design, monitor, secure, and optimize their operations. If a company is building a new factory, a semiconductor fab, or an automated warehouse — or reshoring production to the US — Rockwell's equipment and software are often what make it run.

**Why the stock is debated:** The business is recovering nicely and has an exciting new tailwind: automating the infrastructure of the AI age (data centers, chip fabs, logistics). But investors have already pushed the price up to about 33x next year's expected earnings — high for a company whose sales still rise and fall with the manufacturing cycle. If the automation boom keeps building, the stock can grow into that price. If the industrial cycle cools, the price could fall meaningfully.

**Key terms explained:**

- **Industrial automation:** The hardware and software that run and optimize factories and production lines — Rockwell's core business.
- **Logix / FactoryTalk:** Logix is Rockwell's family of industrial controllers; FactoryTalk is its software suite for running and analyzing operations.
- **Three segments:** *Intelligent Devices* (the physical hardware), *Software & Control* (controllers and software — the highest-margin part), *Lifecycle Services* (consulting, integration, cybersecurity, maintenance).
- **Segment margin:** A measure of how profitable each segment's sales are; Rockwell's total segment margin rose to 20.7%, a big improvement.
- **Adjusted vs GAAP EPS:** Adjusted EPS (\$10.53 in FY2025) strips out one-off items like restructuring; GAAP EPS includes them. Companies guide on adjusted EPS.
- **Book-to-bill / orders:** New orders versus revenue billed — above 1.0 signals a growing backlog and future growth.
